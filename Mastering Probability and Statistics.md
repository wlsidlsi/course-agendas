# Probability and Statistics Syllabus Outline

**Week 1: Introduction to Probability**
*   Basic Definitions: Sample Space, Events, Probability
*   Axioms of Probability
*   Conditional Probability
*   Independence of Events
*   Bayes' Theorem

**Week 2: Discrete Random Variables**
*   Random Variables: Definition and Types
*   Probability Mass Function (PMF)
*   Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF)
*   Expected Value and Variance
*   Bernoulli and Binomial Distributions

**Week 3: More Discrete Distributions**
*   Geometric Distribution
*   Poisson Distribution
*   Hypergeometric Distribution
*   Applications of Discrete Distributions

**Week 4: Continuous Random Variables**
*   Probability Density Function (PDF)
*   Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF)
*   Expected Value and Variance
*   Uniform Distribution
*   Exponential Distribution

**Week 5: Normal Distribution**
*   Normal Distribution: Properties and Standard Normal
*   Calculating Probabilities with Normal Distribution
*   Normal Approximation to the Binomial
*   Central Limit Theorem (Introduction)

**Week 6: Joint Distributions**
*   Joint Probability Distributions (Discrete and Continuous)
*   Marginal Distributions
*   Conditional Distributions
*   Independence of Random Variables

**Week 7: Functions of Random Variables**
*   Expected Value of Functions of Random Variables
*   Variance of Functions of Random Variables
*   Covariance and Correlation
*   Sum of Independent Random Variables

**Week 8: Introduction to Statistics**
*   Populations and Samples
*   Descriptive Statistics: Measures of Central Tendency and Dispersion
*   Data Visualization: Histograms, Boxplots, Scatterplots

**Week 9: Sampling Distributions**
*   Sampling Distribution of the Sample Mean
*   Central Limit Theorem (Formal Statement)
*   Sampling Distribution of the Sample Proportion

**Week 10: Estimation**
*   Point Estimation: Properties of Estimators
*   Bias, Variance, Mean Squared Error (MSE)
*   Confidence Intervals: Construction and Interpretation (z-intervals)

**Week 11: More Estimation**
*   Confidence Intervals: t-intervals
*   Confidence Intervals: Population Proportion
*   Sample Size Determination

**Week 12: Hypothesis Testing**
*   Hypothesis Testing: Null and Alternative Hypotheses
*   Type I and Type II Errors
*   P-value Approach

**Week 13: Hypothesis Testing: One Sample**
*   Hypothesis Testing: One Sample z-test
*   Hypothesis Testing: One Sample t-test
*   Hypothesis Testing: One Sample Proportion

**Week 14: Hypothesis Testing: Two Samples**
*   Hypothesis Testing: Two Sample z-test (Independent Samples)
*   Hypothesis Testing: Two Sample t-test (Independent Samples)
*   Hypothesis Testing: Paired t-test

**Week 15: Chi-Square Tests**
*   Chi-Square Goodness-of-Fit Test
*   Chi-Square Test of Independence

**Week 16: Linear Regression**
*   Simple Linear Regression: Model and Assumptions
*   Estimating Regression Parameters
*   Inference in Regression
*   Correlation and Regression
