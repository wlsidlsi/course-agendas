**I. Thermodynamics Fundamentals**

*   Understanding Basic Thermodynamic Concepts
    *   Defining system, surroundings, and boundary.
    *   Understanding thermodynamic properties (pressure, temperature, volume, energy).
    *   Differentiating between intensive and extensive properties.
    *   Understanding thermodynamic equilibrium (thermal, mechanical, chemical).
*   Understanding Thermodynamic Processes
    *   Defining thermodynamic processes: isothermal, adiabatic, isobaric, isochoric.
    *   Analyzing process diagrams (P-V, T-S diagrams).
    *   Understanding reversible and irreversible processes.
*   Understanding the First Law of Thermodynamics
    *   Defining internal energy, heat, and work.
    *   Applying the first law to closed systems: `ΔU = Q - W`.
    *   Applying the first law to open systems (control volumes): steady-flow energy equation.
    *   Solving problems involving heat transfer and work done during various processes.
*   Understanding the Second Law of Thermodynamics
    *   Defining entropy and its relation to the second law.
    *   Understanding the Carnot cycle and its efficiency.
    *   Calculating entropy changes for various processes.
    *   Understanding the concept of irreversibility and its impact on efficiency.

**II. Combustion Processes**

*   Understanding Combustion Chemistry
    *   Defining fuel, oxidizer, and products of combustion.
    *   Understanding stoichiometry and balancing chemical equations.
    *   Calculating air-fuel ratio (AFR) and equivalence ratio (Φ).
    *   Understanding complete and incomplete combustion.
*   Understanding Combustion Thermodynamics
    *   Defining enthalpy of formation and enthalpy of combustion.
    *   Calculating heating values (higher heating value HHV, lower heating value LHV).
    *   Applying the first law to combustion processes.
    *   Calculating adiabatic flame temperature.
*   Understanding Combustion Kinetics
    *   Understanding reaction rates and activation energy.
    *   Understanding chain reactions and radical formation.
    *   Understanding flame propagation and flame speed.
*   Analyzing Combustion in Engines
    *   Understanding premixed and diffusion flames.
    *   Analyzing the stages of combustion in a gasoline engine: spark ignition, flame kernel development, flame propagation, and afterburning.
    *   Understanding knock and pre-ignition.

**III. Gasoline Engine Fundamentals**

*   Understanding Engine Components
    *   Identifying and describing the function of major engine components: cylinder, piston, connecting rod, crankshaft, valves, camshaft, spark plug, intake manifold, exhaust manifold.
*   Understanding Engine Operating Principles
    *   Describing the four strokes of a four-stroke engine: intake, compression, combustion (power), exhaust.
    *   Understanding valve timing and its effects on engine performance.
    *   Defining bore, stroke, displacement, compression ratio.
*   Understanding Engine Performance Parameters
    *   Defining indicated power, brake power, friction power, and mechanical efficiency.
    *   Understanding torque, power, and specific fuel consumption (SFC).
    *   Analyzing engine performance maps.
*   Understanding Engine Types
    *   Differentiating between two-stroke and four-stroke engines.
    *   Understanding different engine configurations: inline, V-engine, opposed-piston engine.

**IV. Gasoline Engine Systems**

*   Understanding the Intake System
    *   Describing the function of the air filter, throttle body, and intake manifold.
    *   Understanding intake manifold design and its effect on engine performance.
    *   Understanding turbocharging and supercharging.
*   Understanding the Fuel System
    *   Describing the function of the fuel tank, fuel pump, fuel filter, fuel injectors, and fuel rail.
    *   Understanding different fuel injection systems: multi-port fuel injection (MPFI), direct injection.
    *   Understanding fuel injection timing and its effects on engine performance and emissions.
*   Understanding the Ignition System
    *   Describing the function of the battery, ignition coil, distributor (or electronic ignition module), and spark plugs.
    *   Understanding ignition timing and its effects on engine performance and emissions.
    *   Understanding different ignition systems: distributor ignition, coil-on-plug ignition.
*   Understanding the Exhaust System
    *   Describing the function of the exhaust manifold, catalytic converter, muffler, and tailpipe.
    *   Understanding catalytic converter operation and its role in emissions reduction.
*   Understanding the Cooling System
    *   Describing the function of the radiator, water pump, thermostat, and coolant.
    *   Understanding the importance of maintaining engine temperature.
*   Understanding the Lubrication System
    *   Describing the function of the oil pump, oil filter, and oil pan.
    *   Understanding the importance of proper lubrication for engine longevity.

**V. Engine Performance and Emissions**

*   Analyzing Factors Affecting Engine Performance
    *   Understanding the effects of air-fuel ratio, compression ratio, spark timing, and valve timing on engine performance.
    *   Understanding the effects of engine speed and load on engine performance.
*   Understanding Engine Emissions
    *   Identifying major engine emissions: carbon monoxide (CO), hydrocarbons (HC), nitrogen oxides (NOx), particulate matter (PM).
    *   Understanding the formation mechanisms of each emission.
    *   Understanding emissions regulations and standards.
*   Analyzing Emissions Control Techniques
    *   Understanding the role of the catalytic converter in reducing emissions.
    *   Understanding exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) and its effects on NOx emissions.
    *   Understanding lean burn and its effects on emissions.
*   Engine Testing and Diagnostics
    *   Using engine dynamometers to measure engine performance.
    *   Using exhaust gas analyzers to measure engine emissions.
    *   Using diagnostic tools (OBD-II scanners) to diagnose engine problems.
