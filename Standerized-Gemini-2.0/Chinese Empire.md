**I. Foundations of Imperial China**

*   Understanding Chinese Geography and its Impact
    *   Major rivers (Yellow, Yangtze): Influence on agriculture and development.
    *   Mountains and deserts: Barriers and trade routes.
*   Overview of Early Chinese Dynasties
    *   Xia, Shang, and Zhou Dynasties: Identifying key characteristics of each.
    *   The Mandate of Heaven: Understanding its significance in legitimizing rule.
*   The Spring and Autumn Period and the Warring States Period
    *   Decline of Zhou authority: Political fragmentation and philosophical development.
    *   Key philosophical schools: Confucianism, Daoism, Legalism - core tenets and influence.
*   Analyzing primary source examples of philosophical thought:
    *   Confucius' *Analects*: Examples of Confucian ethics and governance.
    *   Lao Tzu's *Tao Te Ching*: Examples of Daoist principles of harmony and non-action.

**II. The Qin Dynasty and Unification**

*   Rise of the Qin State
    *   Legalist reforms: Implementing strict laws and centralized control.
    *   Military expansion: Conquering rival states.
*   Qin Shi Huangdi: The First Emperor
    *   Centralization of power: Standardizing weights, measures, and currency.
    *   Construction projects: The Great Wall, Terracotta Army, and their significance.
*   Fall of the Qin Dynasty
    *   Reasons for its collapse: Excessive taxation, forced labor, and popular rebellion.
    *   Chen Sheng and Wu Guang uprising: Case study of early rebellion.

**III. The Han Dynasty: Consolidation and Expansion**

*   The Founding of the Han Dynasty
    *   Liu Bang (Emperor Gaozu): Overthrowing the Qin and establishing Han rule.
    *   Early Han policies: Balancing Legalism with Confucianism.
*   The Reign of Emperor Wu (Wu Di)
    *   Expansion of the empire: Conquests in Central Asia and Korea.
    *   Establishing the Silk Road: Trade and cultural exchange with the West.
    *   Making Confucianism the state ideology: Establishing imperial academies.
*   Han Society and Culture
    *   The Civil Service Examination: Analyzing its impact on social mobility and governance.
    *   Technological advancements: Paper, seismograph, and their impact.
    *   Family structure and social hierarchy: Role of Confucian values.
*   Decline and Fall of the Han Dynasty
    *   Internal problems: Court intrigues, peasant uprisings (Yellow Turban Rebellion).
    *   The Three Kingdoms period: Fragmentation and civil war.

**IV. Sui and Tang Dynasties: Reunification and Golden Age**

*   Reunification under the Sui Dynasty
    *   Emperor Wen (Yang Jian): Restoring centralized rule.
    *   Grand Canal Construction: Purpose and impact on trade and transportation.
    *   Reasons for Sui Dynasty's Short Lifespan
*   The Tang Dynasty: A Golden Age
    *   Emperor Taizong: Effective governance and expansion of the empire.
    *   The Examination System: Enhancing meritocracy and bureaucratic efficiency.
    *   Tang Dynasty Culture and Arts
        *   Poetry (Li Bai, Du Fu): Examples of Tang poetry and their themes.
        *   Buddhism's influence: Development of Chan (Zen) Buddhism.
    *   Empress Wu Zetian: Analyzing her rise to power and controversial reign.
*   Decline of the Tang Dynasty
    *   The An Lushan Rebellion: Impact on Tang power and stability.
    *   Weakening of central authority: Rise of regional warlords.

**V. Song, Yuan, Ming, and Qing Dynasties: Innovation, Foreign Rule, and Late Imperial China**

*   The Song Dynasty: Economic and Cultural Advancements
    *   Economic revolution: Increased trade, urbanization, and technological innovation.
        *   Paper money, gunpowder, and printing technology.
    *   Neo-Confucianism: Zhu Xi's synthesis and its influence on society and governance.
    *   Military weakness: Constant threat from nomadic tribes (Liao, Jin).
*   The Yuan Dynasty: Mongol Rule
    *   Genghis Khan and Kublai Khan: Mongol conquests and establishment of the Yuan Dynasty.
    *   Marco Polo's travels: Assessing the accuracy and impact of his accounts.
    *   Cultural and social changes under Mongol rule: Impact on Chinese traditions.
*   The Ming Dynasty: Restoration of Chinese Rule
    *   Zhu Yuanzhang (Emperor Hongwu): Overthrowing the Yuan and establishing Ming rule.
    *   The voyages of Zheng He: Exploring Southeast Asia, India, and Africa.
    *   Strengthening the Great Wall: Defense against Mongol incursions.
    *   Forbidden City Construction: Symbolism and Function
*   The Qing Dynasty: Manchu Rule and Decline
    *   The Manchu conquest: Reasons for success and establishment of Qing rule.
    *   Kangxi and Qianlong Emperors: Expansion of the empire and cultural achievements.
    *   The Opium Wars: Analyzing the causes and consequences.
        *   Treaty of Nanjing: Impact on Chinese sovereignty and trade.
    *   Internal Rebellions: Taiping Rebellion, Boxer Rebellion - Causes and Consequences.
*   Analyzing Primary Sources from the Qing Dynasty:
    *   Imperial edicts: Examples of Qing imperial policies.
    *   Accounts of foreign encounters: Perspectives on trade and diplomacy.
