**I. Foundations of the Indian Empire**

*   Understanding the Geographical Context of the Indian Subcontinent
    *   Analyzing the influence of the Himalayas, the Indus and Ganges river systems, and the Deccan Plateau on Indian history and development.
    *   Locating key regions on a map: Punjab, Bengal, the Gangetic Plain, the Deccan, and the coastal regions.

*   Exploring the Pre-Mauryan Period
    *   Investigating the Indus Valley Civilization (Harappan Civilization): key cities (e.g., Harappa, Mohenjo-daro), social organization, and decline.
    *   Examining the Vedic Period: the arrival of the Indo-Aryans, the development of the caste system, the composition of the Vedas (Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, Atharvaveda).
    *   Understanding the rise of the Mahajanapadas: key kingdoms like Magadha, Kosala, and Avanti; political structures and social dynamics.

**II. The Mauryan Empire (c. 322 - 185 BCE)**

*   Analyzing the Rise of Chandragupta Maurya
    *   Understanding the role of Chanakya (Kautilya) in establishing the Mauryan Empire.
    *   Studying the text *Arthashastra* and its principles of statecraft, economics, and military strategy.
    *   Examining Chandragupta's military campaigns and consolidation of power.

*   Exploring the Reign of Ashoka the Great
    *   Understanding the Kalinga War and its impact on Ashoka's policies.
    *   Analyzing Ashoka's Dhamma (Pillars Edicts, Rock Edicts, Minor Rock Edicts): principles of non-violence, religious tolerance, and social welfare.
    *   Investigating the spread of Buddhism during Ashoka's reign.

*   Understanding Mauryan Administration and Society
    *   Examining the centralized administrative system: provincial governors, district officials, and village administration.
    *   Analyzing the Mauryan economy: agriculture, trade, taxation, and currency.
    *   Understanding Mauryan art and architecture: stupas (Sanchi), pillars, and sculptures.
    *   Analyzing factors contributing to the decline of the Mauryan Empire.

**III. Post-Mauryan Period (c. 185 BCE - 320 CE)**

*   Investigating the Rise of the Sunga Dynasty
    *   Understanding the overthrow of the Mauryan dynasty by Pushyamitra Sunga.
    *   Examining the Sunga dynasty's policies and its contribution to art and culture.

*   Exploring the Satavahana Dynasty
    *   Analyzing the Satavahana dynasty's rule in the Deccan region.
    *   Investigating their role in promoting trade and cultural exchange.

*   Understanding the Indo-Greek Kingdoms
    *   Examining the establishment of Indo-Greek kingdoms in Northwest India.
    *   Investigating the influence of Greek culture on Indian art and coinage.
    *   Understanding key figures like Menander (Milinda).

*   Analyzing the Saka and Kushan Empires
    *   Understanding the establishment and expansion of the Saka (Indo-Scythian) and Kushan empires.
    *   Investigating the reign of Kanishka: his patronage of Buddhism and promotion of the Gandhara School of Art.
    *   Analyzing the role of the Silk Road in the Kushan economy and cultural exchange.

**IV. The Gupta Empire (c. 320 - 550 CE)**

*   Analyzing the Rise of the Gupta Dynasty
    *   Understanding the consolidation of power by Chandragupta I and Samudragupta.
    *   Investigating Samudragupta's military campaigns and his image as a "warrior king."

*   Exploring the Reign of Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya)
    *   Understanding the Golden Age of the Gupta Empire: advancements in science, mathematics, literature, and art.
    *   Investigating the Navaratnas (Nine Jewels) of Chandragupta II's court.
    *   Analyzing the accounts of Fa Hien, a Chinese Buddhist monk, about Gupta society.

*   Understanding Gupta Administration, Society, and Culture
    *   Examining the decentralized administrative system: role of local rulers and village assemblies.
    *   Analyzing the Gupta economy: agriculture, trade, and land grants.
    *   Investigating Gupta art and architecture: Ajanta and Ellora caves, Nalanda University.
    *   Studying Gupta literature: Kalidasa's *Shakuntala* and other works.
    *   Analyzing the development of mathematics and astronomy during the Gupta period (Aryabhata, Brahmagupta).

*   Analyzing the Decline of the Gupta Empire
    *   Understanding the Huna invasions and their impact on the Gupta dynasty.
    *   Investigating internal factors contributing to the empire's disintegration.

**V.  Legacy of the Indian Empires**

*   Evaluating the long-term impact of the Mauryan Empire on Indian politics and society.
*   Analyzing the cultural contributions of the Gupta Empire and its lasting influence on Indian art, literature, and science.
*   Comparing and contrasting the administrative and economic systems of the Mauryan and Gupta empires.
*   Discussing the role of religion (Buddhism, Hinduism) in shaping the course of Indian history during these periods.
