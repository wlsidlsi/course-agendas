**I. Firearm Safety Fundamentals**

*   Understanding the Four Rules of Gun Safety
    *   Treat every firearm as if it is loaded.
    *   Never point a firearm at anything you are not willing to destroy.
    *   Keep your finger off the trigger until you are ready to fire.
    *   Be sure of your target and what is beyond it.
*   Identifying Common Firearm Components
    *   Action: The working mechanism of a firearm.
    *   Barrel: The metal tube through which a projectile travels.
    *   Stock: The part of the firearm held against the shoulder.
    *   Trigger: The mechanism that initiates the firing sequence.
*   Safe Storage Practices
    *   Using gun safes and lock boxes.
    *   Storing ammunition separately.
    *   Childproofing and preventing unauthorized access.

**II. Firearm Types and Mechanics**

*   Handguns
    *   Revolvers: Understanding single-action and double-action mechanisms.
    *   Semi-automatic pistols: Magazine-fed, self-reloading operation.
*   Long Guns
    *   Rifles: Bolt-action, semi-automatic, lever-action mechanisms.
    *   Shotguns: Pump-action, semi-automatic, break-action mechanisms; gauge vs. caliber.
*   Ammunition
    *   Understanding cartridge components (bullet, case, powder, primer).
    *   Caliber and gauge designations.
    *   Different bullet types and their applications (e.g., FMJ, hollow point).

**III. Basic Firearm Operation and Handling**

*   Loading and Unloading Procedures
    *   Safe loading and unloading of handguns (revolvers and semi-automatics).
    *   Safe loading and unloading of rifles and shotguns.
*   Proper Grip and Stance
    *   Handgun grip techniques (e.g., isosceles, Weaver).
    *   Rifle and shotgun stance (e.g., bladed stance, squared stance).
*   Sight Alignment and Sight Picture
    *   Understanding the relationship between front sight, rear sight, and target.
    *   Focusing on the front sight.
*   Trigger Control
    *   Smooth, consistent trigger pull.
    *   Avoiding jerking the trigger.

**IV. Marksmanship Fundamentals**

*   Breathing Control
    *   Inhaling, exhaling, and holding breath for stability.
*   Target Acquisition
    *   Quickly and accurately finding the target through the sights.
*   Follow-Through
    *   Maintaining sight alignment and trigger control after the shot.
*   Dry Firing Practice
    *   Practicing trigger control and sight alignment without live ammunition.
    *   Using snap caps to protect the firearm's firing pin.

*   Disassembly and Reassembly
    *   Safe disassembly and reassembly procedures for common firearms.

**VI. Legal and Ethical Considerations**
