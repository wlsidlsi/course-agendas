**I. Introduction to Mesopotamia**

*   Defining Mesopotamia: "The Land Between Two Rivers"
    *   Geographic boundaries: Tigris and Euphrates river systems, Fertile Crescent.
    *   Modern-day locations: Primarily Iraq, with parts of Syria, Turkey, and Iran.
*   Chronology of Mesopotamian Civilizations
    *   Early Bronze Age: Sumerian city-states.
    *   Middle Bronze Age: Akkadian Empire, Old Babylonian Empire.
    *   Late Bronze Age: Assyrian Empire, Kassite Dynasty.
    *   Iron Age: Neo-Assyrian Empire, Neo-Babylonian Empire.
*   Sources for Studying Mesopotamia
    *   Archaeological excavations and artifacts.
    *   Cuneiform texts: Clay tablets, inscriptions (e.g., the Epic of Gilgamesh, Code of Hammurabi).
    *   Secondary scholarly sources: Histories, analyses, and interpretations.

**II. Sumerian City-States (c. 4500-2300 BCE)**

*   Origins and Development of Sumerian Civilization
    *   Ubaid period: Early settlements and agricultural practices.
    *   Emergence of city-states: Uruk, Ur, Lagash, Kish.
*   Political Organization and Social Structure
    *   Theocracy: Role of the *ensi* (priest-king).
    *   Social hierarchy: Priests, nobles, artisans, farmers, slaves.
*   Economic Activities
    *   Agriculture: Irrigation, crop cultivation (barley, wheat), animal husbandry.
    *   Trade: Mesopotamian trade networks with neighboring regions.
    *   Craftsmanship: Pottery, metalworking, textile production.
*   Religious Beliefs and Practices
    *   Polytheism: Pantheon of Sumerian gods and goddesses (e.g., Anu, Enlil, Enki, Inanna).
    *   Ziggurats: Temple complexes dedicated to the city's patron deity.
    *   Rituals and offerings: Religious festivals and ceremonies.
*   Technological Innovations
    *   Wheel: Development and use in transportation and pottery making.
    *   Cuneiform writing: Origin and evolution of the Sumerian script.
    *   Mathematics: Sexagesimal system (base 60).

**III. The Akkadian Empire (c. 2334-2154 BCE)**

*   Sargon of Akkad: Unification of Sumerian city-states.
    *   Military campaigns and territorial expansion.
    *   Establishment of the Akkadian Empire: First empire in Mesopotamia.
*   Administration and Governance
    *   Centralized bureaucracy: Akkadian language as the official language.
    *   Standardization of weights and measures.
    *   Appointment of Akkadian governors in conquered territories.
*   Akkadian Culture and Art
    *   Adoption of Sumerian cuneiform writing for Akkadian language.
    *   Development of Akkadian literature and poetry.
    *   Sculptural achievements: Head of Sargon, Victory Stele of Naram-Sin.
*   Decline and Fall of the Akkadian Empire
    *   Internal rebellions and conflicts.
    *   Gutian invasions and the collapse of Akkadian authority.

**IV. The Old Babylonian Empire (c. 1894-1595 BCE)**

*   Rise of Babylon under Hammurabi
    *   Military conquests and expansion of Babylonian territory.
    *   Establishment of Babylon as a major political and cultural center.
*   The Code of Hammurabi
    *   Content and structure of the law code.
        *   Laws regulating social behavior, family life, commerce, and property.
        *   Principles of justice and punishment (e.g., *lex talionis* - "an eye for an eye").
    *   Significance as a historical and legal document.
*   Babylonian Society and Economy
    *   Social classes: *Awilu* (free citizens), *Mushkenu* (dependent persons), *Wardu* (slaves).
    *   Economic activities: Agriculture, trade, craftsmanship.
    *   Development of Babylonian mathematics and astronomy.
*   Decline and Fall of the Old Babylonian Empire
    *   Internal political instability.
    *   Hittite sack of Babylon.

**V. The Assyrian Empires (c. 2500-609 BCE)**

*   The Old Assyrian Period
    *   Rise of Assur and establishment of trading colonies in Anatolia.
*   The Middle Assyrian Period
    *   Consolidation of Assyrian power under rulers like Ashur-uballit I.
*   The Neo-Assyrian Empire (911-609 BCE)
    *   Military Expansion and Conquests
        *   Aggressive military campaigns under rulers such as Ashurnasirpal II, Tiglath-Pileser III, and Sargon II.
        *   Conquest of vast territories: Mesopotamia, Syria, Palestine, Egypt.
    *   Assyrian Military Organization and Tactics
        *   Well-organized army: Infantry, cavalry, chariots.
        *   Siege warfare: Use of siege engines and fortifications.
        *   Deportation of conquered populations.
    *   Assyrian Administration and Governance
        *   Centralized bureaucracy: Governors and officials appointed by the king.
        *   Construction of monumental palaces and cities (e.g., Nimrud, Nineveh).
        *   Collection of tribute from conquered territories.
    *   Assyrian Culture and Art
        *   Development of Assyrian art and architecture: Relief sculptures, monumental statues.
        *   Preservation of knowledge: Library of Ashurbanipal.
        *   Use of cuneiform writing and Mesopotamian literary traditions.
    *   Decline and Fall of the Neo-Assyrian Empire
        *   Overextension of the empire and internal rebellions.
        *   Coalition of Babylonians, Medes, and Scythians against Assyria.
        *   Sack of Nineveh and the collapse of Assyrian power.

**VI. The Neo-Babylonian Empire (626-539 BCE)**

*   Rise of Babylon under Nabopolassar
    *   Alliance with the Medes and destruction of the Assyrian Empire.
*   Nebuchadnezzar II and the Babylonian Renaissance
    *   Military campaigns and expansion of Babylonian territory.
    *   Rebuilding of Babylon: Ishtar Gate, Hanging Gardens.
    *   Babylonian Captivity of the Jews.
*   Babylonian Culture and Science
    *   Advancements in astronomy and mathematics.
    *   Development of Babylonian literature and art.
*   Decline and Fall of the Neo-Babylonian Empire
    *   Internal political instability.
    *   Conquest of Babylon by Cyrus the Great of Persia.

**VII. Mesopotamian Legacy**

*   Influence on subsequent civilizations
    *   Legal codes: Influence on later legal systems.
    *   Writing system: Cuneiform as a precursor to alphabetic scripts.
    *   Astronomy and mathematics: Foundations of scientific knowledge.
    *   Literature and mythology: Influence on Western literature and religious traditions.
*   Contemporary relevance
    *   Understanding the origins of urban civilization and state formation.
    *   Insights into human behavior, social organization, and cultural development.
    *   Appreciation of the achievements and complexities of ancient Mesopotamian societies.
