**I. Foundations of the Roman Republic (509-27 BCE)**

*   Understanding Roman Geography and its Impact
    *   Location on the Italian Peninsula: access to sea trade, fertile land.
    *   The Seven Hills of Rome: strategic defense advantages.
*   The Myth of Romulus and Remus
    *   Exploring the legendary founding of Rome.
    *   Analyzing the symbolic importance of the myth.
*   Early Roman Society
    *   Patricians vs. Plebeians: Social hierarchy and conflict.
    *   The role of the family and `paterfamilias`.
*   The Roman Constitution
    *   The Senate: Composition, powers, and influence.
    *   Consuls: Election, term limits, and responsibilities.
    *   Assemblies: Comitia Centuriata, Comitia Tributa, Concilium Plebis.
    *   The Twelve Tables: Codification of Roman Law.
*   Expansion and Conquest
    *   The Punic Wars: Conflicts with Carthage (First, Second, Third).
        *   Hannibal's invasion of Italy and the Battle of Cannae.
        *   Scipio Africanus and the Battle of Zama.
    *   Expansion into Greece and the Eastern Mediterranean.

**II. The Late Republic and its Crises (133-27 BCE)**

*   Social and Economic Problems
    *   Decline of the small farmer: Land concentration and the rise of latifundia.
    *   Urbanization and unemployment in Rome.
    *   Slave revolts: Spartacus's rebellion.
*   Political Instability
    *   The Gracchi Brothers: Attempts at land reform and their assassinations.
    *   Marius and Sulla: Military reforms and civil war.
        *   Marius's reforms and the creation of a professional army.
        *   Sulla's dictatorship and its consequences.
    *   The First Triumvirate: Caesar, Pompey, and Crassus.
*   Julius Caesar and the End of the Republic
    *   Caesar's conquest of Gaul: Its impact on his popularity and power.
    *   Caesar's Civil War: Conflict with Pompey and the Senate.
        *   Crossing the Rubicon.
        *   The Battle of Pharsalus.
    *   Caesar's dictatorship and reforms.
    *   Caesar's assassination and its aftermath.
*   The Second Triumvirate: Octavian, Antony, and Lepidus
    *   Division of power and territories.
    *   The Battle of Philippi: Defeat of Caesar's assassins.
    *   Conflict between Octavian and Antony: The Battle of Actium.

**III. The Roman Empire (27 BCE - 476 CE/1453 CE)**

*   The Principate: Augustus and the Pax Romana
    *   Octavian becomes Augustus: The transition from Republic to Empire.
    *   Augustus's reforms: Administration, military, and social policies.
    *   The Julio-Claudian Dynasty: Tiberius, Caligula, Claudius, Nero.
*   The Flavian Dynasty: Vespasian, Titus, Domitian
    *   The Year of the Four Emperors: Instability after Nero's death.
    *   Vespasian and the construction of the Colosseum.
    *   The eruption of Mount Vesuvius and the destruction of Pompeii.
*   The "Five Good Emperors": Nerva, Trajan, Hadrian, Antoninus Pius, Marcus Aurelius
    *   Territorial expansion under Trajan.
    *   Hadrian's Wall and the consolidation of frontiers.
    *   Marcus Aurelius and Stoic philosophy.
*   The Crisis of the Third Century (235-284 CE)
    *   Political instability: Frequent changes in emperors.
    *   Economic problems: Inflation, trade disruptions.
    *   Military pressures: Barbarian invasions and internal revolts.
*   The Dominate: Diocletian and the Tetrarchy
    *   Diocletian's reforms: Division of the Empire into East and West.
    *   The Tetrarchy: System of four rulers (two Augusti and two Caesars).
    *   Persecution of Christians.
*   Constantine and the Christianization of the Empire
    *   Constantine's rise to power: The Battle of the Milvian Bridge.
    *   The Edict of Milan: Religious toleration.
    *   The Council of Nicaea: Establishing Christian orthodoxy.
    *   The founding of Constantinople.
*   The Decline and Fall of the Western Roman Empire
    *   Theodosius and the division of the Empire.
    *   Barbarian migrations: Visigoths, Vandals, Huns.
    *   The sack of Rome by the Visigoths (410 CE).
    *   The deposition of Romulus Augustulus (476 CE).
*   The Byzantine Empire (Eastern Roman Empire)
    *   Continuation of Roman traditions and culture in the East.
    *   Justinian and his accomplishments: Law code (`Corpus Juris Civilis`), reconquest of territories, construction of Hagia Sophia.
    *   The Great Schism (1054): Separation of the Eastern Orthodox Church from the Roman Catholic Church.
    *   The fall of Constantinople to the Ottoman Turks (1453).

**V. Legacy of Rome**

*   Influence on Western Civilization
    *   Language: Latin roots in Romance languages.
    *   Law: Principles of Roman law in modern legal systems.
    *   Political thought: Concepts of republic, empire, citizenship.
    *   Art and architecture: Inspiration for Renaissance and Neoclassical styles.
*   Preservation of Roman Heritage
    *   Archaeological sites: Pompeii, Herculaneum, Roman Forum.
    *   Museum collections of Roman artifacts.
    *   Scholarly research and publications on Roman history and culture.
