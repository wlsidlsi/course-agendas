# Egyptian Empire: Course Syllabus

**Week 1: Introduction to Egypt & Geography**
*   Defining Ancient Egypt: Chronology, Geography, and Sources.
*   The Nile River: Its Impact on Egyptian Civilization.
*   Predynastic Egypt: Cultural Development and Early Social Structures.

**Week 2: Early Dynastic Period & Old Kingdom**
*   Unification of Upper and Lower Egypt.
*   The Rise of the Pharaoh: Divine Kingship.
*   Old Kingdom Society: Social Hierarchy, Administration, and Economy.
*   Pyramid Construction: Techniques, Labor, and Religious Significance.

**Week 3: First Intermediate Period & Middle Kingdom**
*   Causes and Consequences of the First Intermediate Period.
*   Reunification and the Rise of the Middle Kingdom.
*   Expansion and Trade: Nubia and the Near East.
*   Middle Kingdom Art, Literature, and Religion.

**Week 4: Second Intermediate Period & Hyksos Invasion**
*   Decline of the Middle Kingdom and Internal Strife.
*   The Hyksos Invasion and Rule.
*   Egyptian Response to the Hyksos: Technological and Military Innovations.

**Week 5: New Kingdom: Early Dynasties & Imperial Expansion**
*   The Liberation of Egypt and the Founding of the New Kingdom.
*   Thutmose III: Military Campaigns and the Egyptian Empire.
*   Akhenaten and the Amarna Revolution: Religious and Artistic Transformation.

**Week 6: New Kingdom: Later Dynasties & International Relations**
*   Tutankhamun and the Restoration of Traditional Religion.
*   Ramesses II: Military Campaigns and Monumental Architecture.
*   Egyptian Interactions with the Hittites, Mitanni, and other Near Eastern Powers.

**Week 7: Third Intermediate Period & Late Period**
*   Decline of the New Kingdom and Internal Instability.
*   Foreign Rule: Libyan Dynasties and the Kushite Kingdom.
*   The Saite Dynasty and a Revival of Egyptian Culture.

**Week 8: Persian and Ptolemaic Egypt**
*   The Persian Conquest of Egypt.
*   Alexander the Great and the Ptolemaic Dynasty.
*   Hellenistic Culture in Egypt: Alexandria and the Library.

**Week 9: Roman Egypt & Legacy**
*   Roman Rule in Egypt.
*   The Development of Coptic Christianity.
*   The Decline of Ancient Egyptian Culture.

**Week 10: Egyptian Religion & Mythology**
*   Overview of Egyptian Gods and Goddesses.
*   Creation Myths and the Afterlife.
*   Temples and Religious Rituals.

**Week 11: Egyptian Art & Architecture**
*   Characteristics of Egyptian Art: Symbolism and Function.
*   Temples, Tombs, and Pyramids: Architectural Styles.
*   Sculpture, Painting, and Relief: Artistic Techniques.

**Week 12: Daily Life in Ancient Egypt**
*   Social Classes and Occupations.
*   Family Life, Education, and Leisure.
*   Food, Clothing, and Personal Adornment.

**Week 13: Hieroglyphs & Writing System**
*   The Development of Hieroglyphic Writing.
*   Decipherment of Hieroglyphs: The Rosetta Stone.
*   Egyptian Literature and Scribes.

**Week 14: Review & Conclusion**
*   Synthesis of Egyptian History and Culture.
*   Egypt's Enduring Legacy and Influence.
*   Final Project Presentations/Review for Final Exam.
