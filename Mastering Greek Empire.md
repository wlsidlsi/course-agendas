### Greek Empire: Zero to Mastery

**Week 1: Introduction & The Bronze Age**

*   What is Ancient Greece? Geography, Chronology, Sources.
*   Minoan Crete: Palaces, Art, and Writing.
*   Mycenaean Greece: Warrior Culture, Linear B, Trojan War (Myth & Reality).
*   The Collapse of the Bronze Age: Theories and Consequences.

**Week 2: The Dark Ages & Archaic Period**

*   The Greek Dark Ages: Loss of Literacy, Population Shifts.
*   Emergence of the Polis (City-State): Structure, Function, Identity.
*   Greek Colonization: Motives, Patterns, Impact.
*   Development of the Alphabet & Early Greek Literature (Homer, Hesiod).

**Week 3: Sparta & Athens: Two Models**

*   Sparta: Military Society, Lycurgan Reforms, Social Structure.
*   Athens: From Monarchy to Democracy, Solon & Cleisthenes.
*   Comparison and Contrast: Spartan and Athenian Values, Institutions.
*   Social Life: Role of Women, Slavery, Daily Life.

**Week 4: The Persian Wars**

*   Causes of the Persian Wars: Ionian Revolt, Persian Expansion.
*   Key Battles: Marathon, Thermopylae, Salamis, Plataea.
*   Significance of the Persian Wars: Athenian Ascendancy, Greek Identity.
*   Herodotus and the Writing of History.

**Week 5: The Age of Pericles & The Athenian Empire**

*   Pericles and Athenian Democracy: Radical Democracy, Civic Building Programs.
*   The Delian League: Formation, Transformation into Athenian Empire.
*   Athenian Culture: Drama (Tragedy & Comedy), Philosophy (Socrates), Art & Architecture (Parthenon).
*   The Peloponnesian War (Prelude).

**Week 6: The Peloponnesian War**

*   Causes of the Peloponnesian War: Athenian Expansion, Spartan Fears.
*   Key Events & Strategies: Archidamian War, Sicilian Expedition.
*   Internal Strife: Athenian Civil War, the Thirty Tyrants.
*   Thucydides and the Historiography of War.

**Week 7: Aftermath of the Peloponnesian War & Rise of Macedon**

*   The Weakening of the Polis System.
*   Thebes and the Rise of Epaminondas.
*   The Reign of Philip II of Macedon: Military Reforms, Conquests.
*   Demosthenes and Opposition to Macedonian Power.

**Week 8: Alexander the Great & The Hellenistic World (Part 1)**

*   Alexander's Conquests: Persia, Egypt, India.
*   Alexander's Legacy: Military Genius, Cultural Impact.
*   The Successor Kingdoms: Ptolemaic Egypt, Seleucid Empire, Antigonid Macedonia.
*   Hellenistic Culture: Koine Greek, Libraries, Museums (Alexandria).

**Week 9: The Hellenistic World (Part 2) & Roman Expansion**

*   Hellenistic Philosophy: Stoicism, Epicureanism, Cynicism.
*   Hellenistic Science & Technology: Mathematics, Astronomy, Medicine.
*   Social and Economic Changes in the Hellenistic World.
*   The Rise of Rome: Early Republic, Expansion in Italy.

**Week 10: Roman Conquest of Greece & Integration**

*   The Macedonian Wars: Roman Intervention in Greek Affairs.
*   The Achaean League & Roman Domination.
*   Greece under Roman Rule: Administration, Economy, Culture.
*   Legacy of Greece on Roman Civilization: Literature, Art, Philosophy.
