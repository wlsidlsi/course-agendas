# Persian Empire: Syllabus Outline

**I. Foundations & Origins**

*   A. Pre-Achaemenid Persia: Geography, Peoples, and Early Kingdoms (Elam, Media)
*   B. Rise of the Achaemenids: Cyrus the Great and Unification of Persia
*   C. Cyrus Cylinder: Propaganda, Legitimacy, and Early Imperial Policy

**II. Imperial Expansion & Consolidation**

*   A. Conquests of Cyrus and Cambyses: Lydia, Babylon, Egypt
*   B. Darius I: Organization and Administration of the Empire
*   C. Satraps, Royal Road, and Imperial Infrastructure

**III. Achaemenid Government & Society**

*   A. Imperial Administration: Centralization vs. Local Autonomy
*   B. Social Structure: Royal Court, Aristocracy, Free Peoples, and Slaves
*   C. Religion: Zoroastrianism and Royal Patronage

**IV. The Persian Wars**

*   A. Causes of Conflict: Ionian Revolt
*   B. Key Battles: Marathon, Thermopylae, Salamis, Plataea
*   C. Consequences of the Wars: Impact on Achaemenid Prestige and Resources

**V. Achaemenid Art, Architecture, and Culture**

*   A. Persepolis: Royal City and Imperial Symbolism
*   B. Achaemenid Art and Sculpture: Influences and Innovations
*   C. Cultural Exchange and Syncretism within the Empire

**VI. Internal Conflicts & Decline**

*   A. Succession Crises and Royal Intrigue
*   B. Satrapal Rebellions and Internal Instability
*   C. Economic Challenges and Social Tensions

**VII. The Fall of the Achaemenids**

*   A. Alexander the Great's Invasion: Strategy and Tactics
*   B. Key Battles: Granicus, Issus, Gaugamela
*   C. Legacy of the Achaemenids: Enduring Influence on the Region

**VIII. Post-Achaemenid Persia**

*   A. The Seleucid Empire: Hellenistic Influence in Persia
*   B. Rise of the Parthians: Establishing a New Persian Dynasty
*   C. Sasanian Empire: Revival of Persian Identity and Power
