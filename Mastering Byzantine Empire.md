# Byzantine Empire: A Journey Through Time

**I. Foundations and Transformation (c. 330-610 AD)**

*   **Week 1:** Late Antiquity & the Rise of Constantine: Diocletian's reforms, Constantine's conversion, founding of Constantinople.
*   **Week 2:** The Theodosian Dynasty & the Barbarian Invasions: Imperial administration, the East-West split, Gothic migrations.
*   **Week 3:** Justinian & Theodora: Codification of law, reconquest of the West, Nika riots, architectural achievements (Hagia Sophia).
*   **Week 4:** The Reign of Justinian: Plague, economic impact, lasting legacy of his reforms.
*   **Week 5:** Challenges and Transformations: Persian Wars, Avar & Slavic incursions, social and economic shifts.

**II. Survival and Recovery (c. 610-1025 AD)**

*   **Week 6:** The Heraclian Dynasty: Reorganization of the empire, Theme system, Monothelitism controversy.
*   **Week 7:** Iconoclasm: Religious and political debates, imperial decrees, impact on society and art.
*   **Week 8:** The Macedonian Renaissance: Recovery and expansion, Basil I, cultural and artistic revival.
*   **Week 9:** Imperial Expansion and Administration: Military successes, diplomacy, expansion into the Balkans and Italy.
*   **Week 10:** The Reign of Basil II: Conquest of Bulgaria, peak of Byzantine power, internal challenges.

**III. Decline and Fall (c. 1025-1453 AD)**

*   **Week 11:** Internal Weakness and External Threats: Succession crises, rise of the aristocracy, Seljuk Turks, Norman incursions.
*   **Week 12:** The Komnenian Emperors: Alexios I Komnenos, the First Crusade, political maneuvering.
*   **Week 13:** The Latin Empire: The Fourth Crusade, sack of Constantinople, establishment of Latin states.
*   **Week 14:** Restoration and Fragmentation: The Palaiologan Dynasty, reconquest of Constantinople, internal divisions.
*   **Week 15:** Final Decline and Ottoman Conquest: Civil wars, economic crisis, Ottoman expansion, fall of Constantinople (1453).

**IV. Legacy and Significance**

*   **Week 16:** Byzantine Art and Architecture: Mosaics, icons, church design, influence on other cultures.
*   **Week 17:** Byzantine Culture and Literature: Preservation of classical knowledge, philosophical debates, historical writing.
*   **Week 18:** Byzantine Law and Administration: Influence on legal systems, imperial bureaucracy, diplomacy.
*   **Week 19:** The Byzantine Legacy: Influence on Eastern Europe, Russia, and the Islamic world.
*   **Week 20:** Review and Discussion: Key themes, debates, and enduring impact of the Byzantine Empire.
