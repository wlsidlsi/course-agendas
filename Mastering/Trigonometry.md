**I. Foundations of Trigonometry**

*   Understanding Angles and Their Measurement
    *   Degrees, minutes, and seconds
    *   Radians: Definition and conversion between degrees and radians (`radians = (degrees * pi) / 180`)
    *   Arc length:  `s = rθ` (where θ is in radians)

*   Trigonometric Functions of Acute Angles
    *   Defining sine, cosine, tangent, cosecant, secant, and cotangent using right triangles.
        *   SOH CAH TOA mnemonic
    *   Reciprocal identities: `sin(θ) = 1/csc(θ)`, `cos(θ) = 1/sec(θ)`, `tan(θ) = 1/cot(θ)`
    *   Quotient identities: `tan(θ) = sin(θ)/cos(θ)`, `cot(θ) = cos(θ)/sin(θ)`
    *   Pythagorean identity: `sin²(θ) + cos²(θ) = 1`
    *   Complementary angle identities (co-function identities): `sin(θ) = cos(90° - θ)`, `tan(θ) = cot(90° - θ)`, `sec(θ) = csc(90° - θ)`
    *   Applying trigonometric functions to solve right triangles: Finding missing sides and angles.

**II. Trigonometric Functions of Any Angle**

*   Angles in Standard Position
    *   Initial side, terminal side, and vertex at the origin.
    *   Positive and negative angles; coterminal angles.
    *   Quadrantal angles (0°, 90°, 180°, 270°, 360°).

*   Defining Trigonometric Functions for Any Angle
    *   Using the unit circle definition: `x = cos(θ)`, `y = sin(θ)`
    *   Reference angles: Finding the acute angle formed by the terminal side of θ and the x-axis.
    *   Determining the sign of trigonometric functions in each quadrant (ASTC rule).

*   Evaluating Trigonometric Functions
    *   Using reference angles and quadrant signs.
    *   Special angles (30°, 45°, 60°): Memorize or derive trigonometric values.
    *   Using calculators (in degree and radian modes).

**III. Graphs of Trigonometric Functions**

*   Understanding Periodic Functions
    *   Definition of a periodic function.
    *   Period, amplitude, and phase shift.

*   Graphing Sine and Cosine Functions
    *   Basic graphs of `y = sin(x)` and `y = cos(x)`.
    *   Transformations: Amplitude changes (`y = A sin(x)`), period changes (`y = sin(Bx)`), phase shifts (`y = sin(x - C)`), and vertical shifts (`y = sin(x) + D`).
    *   Determining the equation of a sine or cosine function from its graph.

*   Graphing Tangent, Cotangent, Secant, and Cosecant Functions
    *   Basic graphs of `y = tan(x)`, `y = cot(x)`, `y = sec(x)`, and `y = csc(x)`.
    *   Identifying asymptotes and key features.
    *   Transformations of tangent and cotangent functions (period changes and phase shifts).

**IV. Trigonometric Identities and Equations**

*   Fundamental Trigonometric Identities
    *   Pythagorean Identities: `sin²(θ) + cos²(θ) = 1`, `1 + tan²(θ) = sec²(θ)`, `1 + cot²(θ) = csc²(θ)`
    *   Sum and Difference Formulas: `sin(A ± B)`, `cos(A ± B)`, `tan(A ± B)`
    *   Double-Angle Formulas: `sin(2θ)`, `cos(2θ)`, `tan(2θ)`
    *   Half-Angle Formulas: `sin(θ/2)`, `cos(θ/2)`, `tan(θ/2)`

*   Verifying Trigonometric Identities
    *   Techniques for manipulating one side of an equation to match the other.
    *   Using algebraic manipulations and fundamental identities.

*   Solving Trigonometric Equations
    *   Basic trigonometric equations: `sin(x) = a`, `cos(x) = b`, `tan(x) = c`
    *   Equations involving multiple angles: `sin(2x) = a`, `cos(3x) = b`
    *   Equations requiring factoring or quadratic formula.
    *   Finding all solutions within a given interval (e.g., 0 to 2π).

**V. Applications of Trigonometry**

*   Law of Sines
    *   Understanding and applying the Law of Sines: `a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C)`
    *   Solving triangles: AAS, ASA, SSA (ambiguous case).

*   Law of Cosines
    *   Understanding and applying the Law of Cosines: `a² = b² + c² - 2bc cos(A)`
    *   Solving triangles: SSS, SAS

*   Area of a Triangle
    *   Using trigonometric formulas to find the area: `Area = (1/2)bc sin(A)`, Heron's formula.

*   Vectors
    *   Representing vectors geometrically and algebraically.
    *   Vector addition and subtraction.
    *   Scalar multiplication.
    *   Dot product and angle between vectors.
    *   Applications of vectors in physics and engineering (e.g., force, velocity).
