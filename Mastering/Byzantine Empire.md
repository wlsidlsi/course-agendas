**I. Origins and Foundations**

*   Understanding the Late Roman Empire
    *   Political Instability: Examining the Crisis of the Third Century and its impact on Roman governance.
    *   Economic Decline: Analyzing the factors contributing to economic stagnation and inflation.
    *   Social Transformations: Understanding changes in social structure and the rise of Christianity.
*   The Rise of Constantinople
    *   Reasons for its Founding: Strategic location, economic advantages, and political considerations.
    *   Constantine the Great: His role in establishing Constantinople as the "New Rome."
    *   Early Development: Infrastructure, population growth, and administrative organization.
*   The Division of the Roman Empire
    *   Theodosius I and his Successors: Understanding the circumstances leading to the permanent split.
    *   West vs. East: Comparing the political, economic, and social trajectories of the two halves.
    *   The Fall of the Western Roman Empire: Analyzing its causes and consequences for the East.

**II. Early Byzantine Empire (4th-7th Centuries)**

*   The Reign of Justinian I (527-565 AD)
    *   Justinian's Goals: Reunifying the Roman Empire, reforming the legal system, and promoting Orthodox Christianity.
    *   Military Campaigns: Understanding the reconquest of North Africa, Italy, and parts of Spain.
    *   The *Corpus Juris Civilis*: Studying the codification of Roman law and its impact on legal systems.
        *   The *Digest*, *Institutes*, *Code*, and *Novellae*.
    *   Architectural Achievements: Examining the construction of the Hagia Sophia and other significant buildings.
*   The Challenges of the 7th Century
    *   The Persian Wars: Analyzing the conflict with the Sasanian Empire and its impact on Byzantine resources.
    *   The Arab Conquests: Understanding the loss of territories in the Levant, Egypt, and North Africa.
    *   Internal Instability: Examining political turmoil and social unrest within the empire.
*   Heraclius and the Recovery
    *   Heraclius' Reforms: Reorganizing the army, reforming the administration, and promoting a "Greek" identity.
    *   The Theme System: Studying the creation of military districts and their role in defending the empire.
    *   Theological Controversies: Understanding the Monothelite controversy and its impact on Byzantine society.

**III. Middle Byzantine Empire (8th-12th Centuries)**

*   The Iconoclast Controversy (726-843 AD)
    *   Arguments for and against Iconoclasm: Analyzing the theological and political issues at stake.
    *   The Role of Emperors and Patriarchs: Understanding the involvement of key figures in the controversy.
    *   The Impact on Byzantine Art and Culture: Examining the destruction and restoration of religious images.
*   The Macedonian Dynasty (867-1056 AD)
    *   Basil I and the Rise of the Macedonians: Establishing the dynasty and consolidating power.
    *   Military Expansion: Reconquering territories in Italy, the Balkans, and the East.
    *   Economic Prosperity: Analyzing the growth of trade, agriculture, and industry.
    *   Cultural Renaissance: Examining the revival of classical learning and artistic achievements.
*   Conflicts and Challenges
    *   The Great Schism (1054): Understanding the split between the Eastern Orthodox and Roman Catholic Churches.
    *   The Seljuk Turks: Analyzing their expansion into Anatolia and the Battle of Manzikert (1071).
    *   Internal Strife: Examining political instability and social tensions within the empire.

**IV. Late Byzantine Empire (13th-15th Centuries)**

*   The Fourth Crusade and the Latin Empire (1204-1261 AD)
    *   Reasons for the Crusade: Analyzing the political and economic motivations behind the attack on Constantinople.
    *   The Sack of Constantinople: Examining the destruction and looting of the city.
    *   The Establishment of the Latin Empire: Understanding its organization and governance.
    *   Byzantine Successor States: Studying the Empires of Nicaea, Trebizond, and the Despotate of Epirus.
*   The Palaiologan Dynasty (1261-1453 AD)
    *   Michael VIII Palaiologos: Recapturing Constantinople and restoring the Byzantine Empire.
    *   Internal Weaknesses: Analyzing the decline of the economy, military, and administration.
    *   The Hesychast Controversy: Understanding the theological debate and its impact on Byzantine society.
*   The Fall of Constantinople (1453 AD)
    *   Ottoman Expansion: Analyzing the rise of the Ottoman Empire and its conquest of Byzantine territories.
    *   The Siege of Constantinople: Examining the military tactics and strategies employed by both sides.
    *   The Aftermath: Understanding the end of the Byzantine Empire and its legacy.

**V. Byzantine Culture and Society**

*   Religion and the Church
    *   The Role of the Patriarch: Understanding the position of the Patriarch of Constantinople and his influence.
    *   Monasticism: Analyzing the importance of monasteries in Byzantine society and culture.
    *   The Byzantine Liturgy: Examining the rituals and ceremonies of the Eastern Orthodox Church.
*   Art and Architecture
    *   Byzantine Mosaics: Studying the techniques and symbolism of Byzantine mosaics.
    *   Byzantine Iconography: Understanding the conventions and meanings of Byzantine icons.
    *   Byzantine Architecture: Analyzing the design and construction of churches, palaces, and fortifications.
*   Literature and Learning
    *   Byzantine Historiography: Examining the works of Byzantine historians such as Procopius and Anna Komnene.
    *   Classical Scholarship: Understanding the preservation and transmission of classical Greek learning.
    *   Byzantine Law: Analyzing the development and application of Byzantine law.

**VI. Byzantine Legacy**

*   Influence on Eastern Europe
    *   Conversion of the Slavs: Understanding the role of Cyril and Methodius in spreading Christianity and Byzantine culture.
    *   Byzantine Influence on Art and Architecture: Examining the adoption of Byzantine styles in Eastern European churches and palaces.
    *   The Cyrillic Alphabet: Studying its origins and development.
*   Influence on the Islamic World
    *   Byzantine Art and Architecture in Islamic Lands: Examining the adoption and adaptation of Byzantine styles.
    *   Byzantine Learning and Scholarship: Understanding the transmission of Byzantine knowledge to the Islamic world.
*   Byzantine Influence on the Renaissance
    *   The Rediscovery of Classical Learning: Understanding the role of Byzantine scholars in bringing Greek manuscripts to Italy.
    *   Byzantine Art and Architecture in Italy: Examining the influence of Byzantine styles on Renaissance art and architecture.
