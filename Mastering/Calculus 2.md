*   Limits and Continuity
    *   Understanding the concept of a limit
    *   Evaluating limits using algebraic techniques
    *   Understanding continuity and its properties
*   Derivatives
    *   Defining the derivative as a limit
    *   Applying differentiation rules (power rule, product rule, quotient rule, chain rule)
    *   Understanding implicit differentiation
*   Applications of Derivatives
    *   Finding critical points and extrema
    *   Applying the first and second derivative tests
    *   Solving optimization problems
*   Integrals
    *   Defining the definite and indefinite integral
    *   Applying the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
    *   Using substitution to evaluate integrals

**II. Techniques of Integration**

*   Integration by Parts
    *   Understanding the integration by parts formula:  ∫u dv = uv - ∫v du
    *   Choosing appropriate `u` and `dv`
    *   Applying integration by parts to various functions (e.g., ∫x sin(x) dx, ∫x^2 e^x dx, ∫ln(x) dx)
*   Trigonometric Integrals
    *   Integrating powers of sine and cosine
        *   Using trigonometric identities to simplify integrals of the form ∫sin^m(x) cos^n(x) dx
    *   Integrating powers of tangent and secant
        *   Using trigonometric identities to simplify integrals of the form ∫tan^m(x) sec^n(x) dx
*   Trigonometric Substitution
    *   Understanding when to use trigonometric substitution (a^2 - x^2, a^2 + x^2, x^2 - a^2)
    *   Performing trigonometric substitutions and evaluating the resulting integrals
        *   Examples: ∫√(a^2 - x^2) dx, ∫√(a^2 + x^2) dx, ∫√(x^2 - a^2) dx
*   Partial Fractions
    *   Factoring the denominator
    *   Decomposing rational functions into partial fractions
        *   Linear factors, repeated linear factors, irreducible quadratic factors
    *   Integrating the resulting partial fractions

**III. Applications of Integration**

*   Area Between Curves
    *   Setting up integrals to find the area between two curves
    *   Integrating with respect to `x` and `y`
*   Volumes
    *   Volumes of solids of revolution
        *   Disk method
        *   Washer method
        *   Shell method
    *   Volumes by cross-sections
*   Arc Length
    *   Deriving and applying the arc length formula:  ∫√(1 + (dy/dx)^2) dx or ∫√(1 + (dx/dy)^2) dy
*   Surface Area
    *   Deriving and applying the surface area formula for solids of revolution:  ∫2πy √(1 + (dy/dx)^2) dx or ∫2πx √(1 + (dx/dy)^2) dy

**IV. Improper Integrals**

*   Discontinuous Integrands
    *   Evaluating integrals with discontinuous integrands within the interval of integration
