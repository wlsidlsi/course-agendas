**I. German Phonetics and Pronunciation**

*   Understanding the German alphabet and its pronunciation
    *   Vowel sounds (long, short, umlauts) - examples: `a`, `e`, `i`, `o`, `u`, `ä`, `ö`, `ü`
    *   Consonant sounds (hard, soft, voiced, unvoiced) - examples: `b`, `d`, `g`, `p`, `t`, `k`, `ch`, `s`, `ß`
    *   Diphthongs - examples: `ei`, `au`, `eu/äu`
*   Mastering German pronunciation rules
    *   Pronunciation of consonant clusters - examples: `sp`, `st`, `pf`, `tsch`
    *   Word stress and intonation patterns
    *   Minimal pairs practice to differentiate similar sounds - examples: `Beet/bitte`, `Ofen/offen`

**II. Basic German Grammar**

*   Understanding German noun genders (der, die, das) and articles
    *   Identifying noun genders through rules and patterns (with exceptions)
    *   Using definite and indefinite articles correctly
*   Learning German cases (Nominativ, Akkusativ, Dativ, Genitiv)
    *   Understanding the functions of each case
    *   Case marking of articles and nouns
    *   Prepositions that govern specific cases - examples: `mit` (Dativ), `für` (Akkusativ), `wegen` (Genitiv)
*   Forming German sentences
    *   Subject-verb-object order (SVO)
    *   Verb conjugation in present tense (Präsens)
    *   Using personal pronouns - examples: `ich`, `du`, `er/sie/es`, `wir`, `ihr`, `sie/Sie`
*   Basic sentence structures and question formation
    *   Yes/No questions
    *   Wh-questions using question words - examples: `wer`, `was`, `wann`, `wo`, `warum`, `wie`
    *   Using modal verbs (können, müssen, sollen, dürfen, wollen, mögen) in sentences
