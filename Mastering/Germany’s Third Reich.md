**I. Historical Context & Ideological Foundations**

*   Understanding the Weimar Republic (1919-1933)
    *   Political instability: frequent changes in government, coalition difficulties
    *   Economic hardship: hyperinflation (1923), Great Depression (1929)
    *   Social discontent: resentment from Treaty of Versailles, rise of extremist ideologies
*   Analyzing the rise of Adolf Hitler and the Nazi Party (NSDAP)
    *   Hitler's early life and influences: Pan-Germanism, anti-Semitism
    *   The Nazi Party's platform: nationalism, racial purity, authoritarianism
    *   Propaganda and manipulation tactics: rallies, scapegoating, promises of national revival
*   Exploring the key elements of Nazi ideology
    *   Racial ideology: Aryan supremacy, anti-Semitism, eugenics
    *   Lebensraum (living space): expansionist goals in Eastern Europe
    *   Führerprinzip (leadership principle): absolute authority of the leader

**II. Consolidation of Power and Establishment of the Regime**

*   Examining Hitler's ascent to power (1933)
    *   The Reichstag fire and its exploitation
    *   The Enabling Act: granting Hitler dictatorial powers
    *   Elimination of political opposition: suppression of communist and socialist parties
*   Analyzing the establishment of a totalitarian state
    *   Gleichschaltung (coordination): Nazification of all aspects of society
    *   Control of media and culture: propaganda, censorship, book burnings
    *   Suppression of dissent: establishment of concentration camps and the Gestapo
*   Understanding the role of key figures in the Nazi regime
    *   Heinrich Himmler: head of the SS and architect of the Holocaust
    *   Joseph Goebbels: Minister of Propaganda
    *   Hermann Göring: Commander-in-Chief of the Luftwaffe and head of the Four Year Plan

**III. Social and Economic Policies**

*   Analyzing Nazi economic policies
    *   Re-armament and militarization: boosting the economy through military spending
    *   Public works projects: reducing unemployment (e.g., Autobahn construction)
    *   Autarky (economic self-sufficiency): reducing reliance on foreign imports
*   Exploring social policies and control
    *   Youth organizations: Hitler Youth and League of German Girls (indoctrination)
    *   The role of women in Nazi society: emphasis on motherhood and domesticity
    *   Racial hygiene policies: forced sterilization, euthanasia programs ("Action T4")
*   Investigating persecution of minorities
    *   Anti-Semitism: Nuremberg Laws (1935), Kristallnacht (1938)
    *   Persecution of Roma, homosexuals, disabled individuals, and political dissidents

**IV. Foreign Policy and World War II**

*   Analyzing Hitler's foreign policy objectives
    *   Revision of the Treaty of Versailles
    *   Anschluss (annexation) of Austria (1938)
    *   Appeasement policy of Britain and France: Munich Agreement (1938)
*   Understanding the outbreak and course of World War II (1939-1945)
    *   Invasion of Poland (1939): start of the war
    *   Blitzkrieg tactics: rapid advances through Europe
    *   The Eastern Front: invasion of the Soviet Union (Operation Barbarossa)
*   Examining the Holocaust (Shoah)
    *   The Wannsee Conference: planning the "Final Solution"
    *   Extermination camps: Auschwitz-Birkenau, Treblinka, Sobibor
    *   The scale and impact of the genocide

**V. Resistance, Defeat, and Legacy**

*   Exploring resistance to the Nazi regime
    *   Internal resistance: White Rose group, July 20 Plot
    *   External resistance: Allied forces, partisan movements
*   Understanding the defeat of Nazi Germany
    *   Allied invasion of Normandy (D-Day)
    *   Soviet advance on Berlin
    *   Hitler's suicide and unconditional surrender
*   Analyzing the legacy of the Third Reich
    *   Nuremberg Trials: prosecution of Nazi war criminals
    *   Denazification efforts in post-war Germany
    *   The ongoing impact of the Holocaust and the fight against extremism
