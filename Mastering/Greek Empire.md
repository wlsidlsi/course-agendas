**I. Ancient Greece: Geography and Early Civilizations**

*   Understanding the geography of Ancient Greece and its influence
    *   Identifying key regions: mainland, islands, and colonies.
    *   Analyzing the impact of mountains and seas on Greek development.
*   Exploring the Minoan and Mycenaean civilizations
    *   Identifying key features of Minoan society on Crete (e.g., Knossos palace, trade).
    *   Analyzing the Mycenaean culture on the mainland (e.g., fortified citadels, Linear B script).
    *   Understanding the possible causes of the collapse of the Mycenaean civilization.

**II. The Archaic Period: Rise of the Polis**

*   Understanding the concept and development of the *polis* (city-state)
    *   Defining the characteristics of a *polis* (e.g., acropolis, agora, citizenship).
    *   Analyzing the factors contributing to the emergence of the *polis* system.
*   Exploring different forms of government in the Archaic Period
    *   Understanding monarchy, aristocracy, oligarchy, and tyranny.
    *   Comparing and contrasting the structures and functions of these forms of government in different *poleis*.
    *   Examples: Athens, Sparta, Corinth, Thebes.
*   Analyzing the development of Greek culture during the Archaic Period
    *   Understanding the development of the Greek alphabet.
    *   Exploring early Greek literature: Homer's *Iliad* and *Odyssey*, Hesiod's *Theogony*.
    *   Understanding the development of Greek religion and mythology.
    *   Understanding the origins of the Olympic Games.

**III. The Classical Period: Athenian Golden Age and Peloponnesian War**

*   Understanding the rise of Athenian democracy
    *   Analyzing the reforms of Solon, Cleisthenes, and Pericles.
    *   Identifying the key institutions of Athenian democracy (e.g., Assembly, Council, Courts).
*   Exploring the Persian Wars
    *   Understanding the causes and major events of the Persian Wars (e.g., Marathon, Thermopylae, Salamis, Plataea).
    *   Analyzing the impact of the Persian Wars on Greek identity and Athenian power.
*   Analyzing the Athenian Golden Age under Pericles
    *   Understanding the expansion of Athenian power and influence.
    *   Exploring Athenian art, architecture, and philosophy (e.g., Parthenon, Socrates, Plato).
*   Understanding the Peloponnesian War
    *   Analyzing the causes of the Peloponnesian War (e.g., Athenian expansionism, Spartan fear).
    *   Identifying the major events and turning points of the war (e.g., Sicilian Expedition).
    *   Understanding the consequences of the Peloponnesian War for Athens and Greece.

**IV. The Hellenistic Period: Alexander the Great and Aftermath**

*   Understanding the rise of Macedon and Alexander the Great
    *   Analyzing the reforms of Philip II of Macedon.
    *   Tracing Alexander's conquests and the creation of his empire.
    *   Understanding Alexander's military strategies and tactics.
*   Exploring the Hellenistic world
    *   Analyzing the spread of Greek culture and language throughout the Eastern Mediterranean.
    *   Understanding the rise of Hellenistic kingdoms (e.g., Ptolemaic Egypt, Seleucid Empire).
    *   Exploring Hellenistic art, science, and philosophy (e.g., Library of Alexandria, Euclid, Archimedes).
*   Understanding the eventual Roman conquest of Greece.
    *   Understanding the slow erosion of Hellenistic power and the gradual absorption into Roman territories.

**V. Ancient Greek Culture and Society**

*   Exploring Ancient Greek religion and mythology
    *   Understanding the Olympian gods and goddesses and their roles in Greek society.
    *   Analyzing the importance of religious festivals and rituals.
*   Understanding Ancient Greek social structure
    *   Analyzing the roles of citizens, women, slaves, and foreigners.
    *   Comparing and contrasting social structures in different *poleis*.
*   Exploring Ancient Greek philosophy and intellectual thought
    *   Understanding the Pre-Socratic philosophers.
    *   Analyzing the teachings of Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle.
    *   Exploring the development of Greek science, mathematics, and medicine.
*   Analyzing Ancient Greek art and architecture
    *   Understanding the different styles of Greek sculpture and pottery.
    *   Exploring the architectural features of Greek temples and public buildings.
*   Exploring Ancient Greek theatre and drama
    *   Understanding the origins of Greek tragedy and comedy.
    *   Analyzing the works of famous playwrights such as Aeschylus, Sophocles, Euripides, and Aristophanes.
