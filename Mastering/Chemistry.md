**I. Foundations of Chemistry**

*   Understanding the Scientific Method
    *   Defining hypothesis, experiment, observation, and conclusion.
    *   Differentiating between qualitative and quantitative data.

*   Matter and Its Properties
    *   Defining matter, mass, and volume.
    *   Distinguishing between physical and chemical properties.
    *   Understanding states of matter: solid, liquid, gas, and plasma.
    *   Performing density calculations: `density = mass / volume`.

*   Classification of Matter
    *   Distinguishing between elements, compounds, and mixtures.
    *   Understanding homogeneous and heterogeneous mixtures.
    *   Separation techniques: filtration, distillation, chromatography.

**II. Atomic Structure and the Periodic Table**

*   The Structure of the Atom
    *   Identifying protons, neutrons, and electrons.
    *   Understanding atomic number, mass number, and isotopes.
    *   Calculating average atomic mass.

*   The Periodic Table
    *   Understanding the organization of the periodic table: groups and periods.
    *   Identifying metals, nonmetals, and metalloids.
    *   Understanding periodic trends: electronegativity, ionization energy, atomic radius.

*   Electronic Configuration
    *   Understanding electron orbitals (s, p, d, f).
    *   Writing electron configurations and orbital diagrams.
    *   Relating electron configuration to the periodic table.

**III. Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure**

*   Types of Chemical Bonds
    *   Understanding ionic bonds: formation and properties.
        *   Example: Formation of `NaCl`.
    *   Understanding covalent bonds: formation and properties.
        *   Example: Formation of `H2O`.
        *   Distinguishing between single, double, and triple bonds.
    *   Understanding metallic bonds: formation and properties.

*   Lewis Structures and VSEPR Theory
    *   Drawing Lewis structures for molecules and polyatomic ions.
    *   Applying VSEPR theory to predict molecular geometry.
    *   Understanding bond angles and molecular polarity.
        *   Examples: Linear, trigonal planar, tetrahedral, bent, trigonal pyramidal.

*   Intermolecular Forces
    *   Understanding dipole-dipole forces, hydrogen bonding, and London dispersion forces.
    *   Relating intermolecular forces to physical properties such as boiling point and melting point.

**IV. Chemical Reactions and Stoichiometry**

*   Chemical Equations
    *   Balancing chemical equations using coefficients.
        *   Example: `H2 + O2 -> H2O`.
    *   Identifying types of chemical reactions: synthesis, decomposition, single replacement, double replacement, combustion.

*   Stoichiometry
    *   Understanding the mole concept and Avogadro's number.
    *   Converting between mass, moles, and number of particles.
    *   Calculating molar mass.
    *   Solving stoichiometric problems involving limiting reactants and percent yield.

*   Reactions in Aqueous Solutions
    *   Understanding solubility rules.
    *   Writing net ionic equations.
    *   Understanding precipitation reactions, acid-base reactions, and redox reactions.

**V. Gases, Liquids, and Solids**

*   The Gas Laws
    *   Understanding Boyle's Law, Charles's Law, Gay-Lussac's Law, and Avogadro's Law.
    *   Applying the Ideal Gas Law: `PV = nRT`.
    *   Understanding Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures.

*   Liquids
    *   Understanding properties of liquids: viscosity, surface tension, vapor pressure.
    *   Understanding phase transitions: evaporation, condensation, boiling.

*   Solids
    *   Understanding types of solids: crystalline and amorphous.
    *   Understanding crystal structures.
    *   Understanding phase diagrams.

**VI. Thermochemistry**

*   Energy and Enthalpy
    *   Understanding the concepts of energy, heat, and work.
    *   Understanding enthalpy and enthalpy changes (ΔH).
    *   Distinguishing between exothermic and endothermic reactions.

*   Calorimetry
    *   Performing calorimetry calculations to determine heat transfer.
    *   Using specific heat capacity: `q = mcΔT`.

*   Hess's Law
    *   Applying Hess's Law to calculate enthalpy changes for reactions.
    *   Using standard enthalpies of formation.

**VII. Chemical Kinetics**

*   Reaction Rates
    *   Understanding factors affecting reaction rates: temperature, concentration, catalysts.
    *   Determining rate laws experimentally.
        *   Understanding rate constants and reaction orders.

*   Reaction Mechanisms
    *   Understanding elementary steps and rate-determining steps.
    *   Proposing reaction mechanisms consistent with experimental data.

*   Catalysis
    *   Understanding homogeneous and heterogeneous catalysis.
    *   Understanding the role of catalysts in increasing reaction rates.

**VIII. Chemical Equilibrium**

*   Equilibrium Constant
    *   Understanding the concept of chemical equilibrium.
    *   Writing equilibrium constant expressions (Kc and Kp).

*   Le Chatelier's Principle
    *   Applying Le Chatelier's Principle to predict the effects of changes in concentration, pressure, and temperature on equilibrium.

*   Acid-Base Equilibria
    *   Understanding acid-base theories: Arrhenius, Bronsted-Lowry, Lewis.
    *   Calculating pH and pOH.
    *   Understanding acid-base titrations.
    *   Understanding buffer solutions.

**IX. Electrochemistry**

*   Redox Reactions
    *   Identifying oxidation and reduction reactions.
    *   Balancing redox reactions using the half-reaction method.

*   Electrochemical Cells
    *   Understanding voltaic cells and electrolytic cells.
    *   Calculating cell potentials using standard reduction potentials.
        *   `E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode`.

*   Electrolysis
    *   Understanding electrolysis of water and molten salts.
    *   Applying Faraday's laws of electrolysis.

**X. Nuclear Chemistry**

*   Radioactivity
    *   Understanding types of radioactive decay: alpha, beta, gamma.
    *   Writing nuclear equations.
    *   Understanding half-life and radioactive dating.

*   Nuclear Reactions
    *   Understanding nuclear fission and nuclear fusion.
    *   Understanding the applications of nuclear chemistry.
