**I. Russian Alphabet and Pronunciation**

*   Learning the Cyrillic Alphabet
    *   Recognizing and writing all 33 letters (uppercase and lowercase).
    *   Understanding the sounds each letter represents, including variations.
        *   Examples: А (a), Б (b), В (v), Г (g), Д (d), Е (ye/e), Ё (yo), Ж (zh), З (z), И (ee), Й (y), К (k), Л (l), М (m), Н (n), О (o), П (p), Р (r), С (s), Т (t), У (u), Ф (f), Х (kh), Ц (ts), Ч (ch), Ш (sh), Щ (shch), Ъ (hard sign), Ы (y - as in "myth"), Ь (soft sign), Э (e), Ю (yu), Я (ya).
*   Mastering Pronunciation Rules
    *   Understanding stressed and unstressed vowels.
        *   Vowel reduction in unstressed syllables: О becomes /a/, Е becomes /i/.
    *   Pronouncing consonant clusters.
        *   Assimilation of voiced and voiceless consonants.
    *   Using pronunciation tools and resources.
        *   Online dictionaries with audio pronunciation (e.g., Forvo).
        *   Language learning apps (e.g., Duolingo, Memrise).

**II. Basic Russian Grammar**

*   Understanding Noun Genders and Declensions
    *   Identifying masculine, feminine, and neuter nouns.
    *   Learning the six cases (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, instrumental, prepositional).
    *   Declining nouns in singular and plural forms across all six cases.
        *   Examples: Table of declensions for a masculine noun like `дом` (house), feminine noun like `книга` (book), and neuter noun like `окно` (window).
*   Learning Verb Conjugation
    *   Understanding the two verb conjugations (first and second conjugation).
    *   Conjugating verbs in the present tense.
        *   Examples: Conjugating `читать` (to read), `говорить` (to speak).
    *   Using pronouns (я, ты, он, она, оно, мы, вы, они).
*   Forming Basic Sentences
    *   Using subject-verb-object order (SVO).
    *   Employing basic prepositions (в, на, у, к, от, до).
        *   Understanding how prepositions affect case usage.
        *   Examples: "Я живу в Москве" (I live in Moscow), "Книга на столе" (The book is on the table).

**III. Essential Vocabulary and Phrases**

*   Greetings and Introductions
    *   Learning common greetings: "Здравствуйте" (Hello), "Привет" (Hi).
    *   Introducing yourself: "Меня зовут..." (My name is...).
    *   Asking "How are you?": "Как дела?"
*   Everyday Expressions
    *   Saying "Please" and "Thank you": "Пожалуйста" (Please/You're welcome), "Спасибо" (Thank you).
    *   Asking for directions: "Где находится...?" (Where is...?).
    *   Ordering food and drinks: "Я хотел(а) бы..." (I would like...).
*   Numbers, Days of the Week, Months, and Time
    *   Counting from 1 to 100.
    *   Reciting the days of the week (понедельник, вторник, среда, четверг, пятница, суббота, воскресенье).
    *   Naming the months of the year (январь, февраль, март, апрель, май, июнь, июль, август, сентябрь, октябрь, ноябрь, декабрь).
    *   Telling time: "Который час?" (What time is it?).

**IV. Reading and Comprehension**

*   Reading Simple Texts
    *   Practicing reading short stories, dialogues, and articles designed for beginners.
    *   Using dictionaries to look up unfamiliar words.
    *   Focusing on understanding the main idea and key details.
*   Developing Reading Strategies
    *   Skimming and scanning for specific information.
    *   Guessing the meaning of words from context.
    *   Identifying cognates (words with similar origins and meanings).
*   Expanding Vocabulary Through Reading
    *   Keeping a vocabulary notebook.
    *   Using flashcards for memorization.
    *   Reviewing new words regularly.

**V. Listening and Speaking**

*   Listening to Audio Resources
    *   Using podcasts, audiobooks, and online videos for language learning.
    *   Practicing active listening by focusing on key words and phrases.
    *   Repeating what you hear to improve pronunciation.
*   Practicing Speaking Skills
    *   Engaging in conversations with native speakers (online or in person).
    *   Recording yourself speaking and listening back to identify areas for improvement.
    *   Participating in language exchange programs.
*   Role-Playing Common Scenarios
    *   Ordering food at a restaurant.
    *   Asking for directions.
    *   Making small talk with a friend.

**VI. Intermediate Grammar and Vocabulary**

*   Past and Future Tenses
    *   Forming the past tense of verbs (using the suffix `-л/-ла/-ло/-ли`).
    *   Expressing the future tense (using the verb `быть` - to be).
        *   Example: Я буду читать (I will read).
*   Aspects of Verbs (Perfective and Imperfective)
    *   Understanding the difference between completed and ongoing actions.
    *   Learning common pairs of perfective and imperfective verbs.
        *   Example: `делать` (to do, imperfective) vs. `сделать` (to do, perfective).
*   Conditional Sentences
    *   Using `если` (if) to express conditions.
    *   Constructing hypothetical situations.
        *   Example: Если будет хорошая погода, мы пойдём гулять (If the weather is good, we will go for a walk).
*   Advanced Vocabulary
    *   Learning synonyms and antonyms.
    *   Using idioms and expressions.
    *   Expanding vocabulary related to specific topics (e.g., travel, culture, current events).

**VII. Cultural Understanding**

*   Exploring Russian Culture and Traditions
    *   Learning about Russian history, literature, and art.
    *   Understanding cultural norms and etiquette.
    *   Exploring Russian cuisine and customs.
*   Understanding Social Context
    *   Learning about Russian values and beliefs.
    *   Understanding the nuances of communication.
    *   Being aware of cultural differences.
*   Immersing Yourself in Russian Media
    *   Watching Russian movies and TV shows.
    *   Listening to Russian music.
    *   Reading Russian newspapers and magazines.
