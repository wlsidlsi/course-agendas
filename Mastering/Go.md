**II. The First Law of Thermodynamics**

*   Defining work and heat
    *   Mechanical work: `W = ∫PdV`
    *   Heat transfer mechanisms: conduction, convection, radiation
    *   Sign conventions for work and heat
*   Understanding internal energy
    *   Internal energy as a state function
    *   Relationship between internal energy, heat, and work: `ΔU = Q - W`
*   Applying the first law to different processes
    *   Isobaric process: `Q = ΔU + PΔV = ΔH` (Enthalpy)
    *   Isochoric process: `Q = ΔU`
    *   Isothermal process: `Q = W` (for ideal gas)
    *   Adiabatic process: `Q = 0; ΔU = -W; PV^γ = constant` (γ = heat capacity ratio)
*   Analyzing cyclic processes
    *   Heat engines and refrigerators
    *   Calculating efficiency of a heat engine: `η = W_net / Q_in`
    *   Coefficient of performance (COP) of a refrigerator: `COP = Q_cold / W_in`

**III. The Second Law of Thermodynamics**

*   Understanding entropy
    *   Entropy as a measure of disorder
    *   Entropy change in reversible and irreversible processes: `dS = dQ/T` (reversible), `dS > dQ/T` (irreversible)
    *   Clausius inequality: `∮ dQ/T ≤ 0`
*   Defining reversible and irreversible processes
    *   Conditions for reversibility: absence of friction, quasi-static process
    *   Real-world processes are irreversible
*   Statements of the second law
    *   Kelvin-Planck statement: No heat engine can convert all heat into work
    *   Clausius statement: No refrigerator can transfer heat from cold to hot without work input
*   Calculating entropy changes
    *   Entropy change for ideal gas: `ΔS = ∫(C_v dT/T) + nRln(V_2/V_1)`
    *   Entropy change during phase transitions: `ΔS = L/T` (L = latent heat)
*   Entropy and the universe
    *   Entropy of an isolated system always increases or remains constant
    *   "The entropy of the universe tends to a maximum"

**IV. Thermodynamic Properties of Pure Substances**

*   Understanding the phase diagram
    *   Triple point and critical point
    *   Solid, liquid, and vapor phases
    *   Phase transitions: melting, vaporization, sublimation
*   Using thermodynamic tables
    *   Saturated liquid and saturated vapor properties
    *   Superheated vapor properties
    *   Compressed liquid properties
*   Equations of state
    *   Ideal gas equation: `PV = nRT`
    *   Van der Waals equation: `(P + a(n/V)^2)(V - nb) = nRT`
    *   Other equations of state: Redlich-Kwong, Soave-Redlich-Kwong
*   Analyzing throttling processes
    *   Joule-Thomson coefficient: `μ_JT = (∂T/∂P)_H`
    *   Applications: refrigeration and liquefaction

**V. Thermodynamic Cycles**

*   Understanding the Carnot cycle
    *   Four reversible processes: isothermal expansion, adiabatic expansion, isothermal compression, adiabatic compression
    *   Carnot efficiency: `η_Carnot = 1 - (T_cold / T_hot)`
    *   Carnot cycle as the most efficient cycle
*   Analyzing power cycles
    *   Rankine cycle: steam power plants
        *   Components: pump, boiler, turbine, condenser
        *   Improving efficiency: reheating, regeneration
    *   Otto cycle: gasoline engines
        *   Four strokes: intake, compression, power, exhaust
        *   Thermal efficiency: `η = 1 - (1/r^(γ-1))` (r = compression ratio)
    *   Diesel cycle: diesel engines
        *   Thermal efficiency depends on compression ratio and cut-off ratio
    *   Brayton cycle: gas turbines
        *   Components: compressor, combustion chamber, turbine
        *   Improving efficiency: regeneration, intercooling, reheating
*   Analyzing refrigeration cycles
    *   Vapor-compression refrigeration cycle
        *   Components: evaporator, compressor, condenser, expansion valve
        *   Coefficient of performance (COP)
    *   Absorption refrigeration cycle
        *   Uses heat as the energy input
        *   Applications: waste heat recovery

    *   Kay's rule for pseudocritical properties
    *   Mixing rules for equations of state
*   Thermodynamics of solutions
    *   Raoult's law: `P_i = x_i P_i^*` (for ideal solutions)
    *   Henry's law: `P_i = H x_i` (for dilute solutions)
*   Chemical potential
    *   Definition and significance
    *   Chemical potential in mixtures

**VII. Chemical Thermodynamics**

*   Understanding chemical reactions
    *   Stoichiometry and balancing chemical equations
    *   Extent of reaction
*   Enthalpy of reaction
    *   Standard enthalpy of formation: `ΔH_f°`
    *   Hess's law
    *   Calculating enthalpy change for reactions at different temperatures: Kirchhoff's equation
*   Entropy of reaction
    *   Standard entropy of formation: `S°`
    *   Calculating entropy change for reactions
*   Gibbs free energy of reaction
    *   Relationship between Gibbs free energy, enthalpy, and entropy: `ΔG = ΔH - TΔS`
    *   Standard Gibbs free energy of formation: `ΔG_f°`
*   Chemical equilibrium
    *   Equilibrium constant: `K = exp(-ΔG°/RT)`
    *   Factors affecting equilibrium: temperature, pressure, concentration
    *   Le Chatelier's principle
