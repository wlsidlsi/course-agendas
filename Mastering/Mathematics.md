**I. Foundations of Mathematics**

*   Number Systems
    *   Understanding natural numbers, integers, rational numbers, irrational numbers, and real numbers.
    *   Representing numbers in different bases (binary, decimal, hexadecimal).
        *   Converting between bases: `decimal_to_binary(10)`, `hexadecimal_to_decimal("A")`.
    *   Complex numbers and their properties.
        *   Understanding the imaginary unit `i`.
        *   Performing arithmetic operations with complex numbers: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

*   Basic Algebra
    *   Solving linear equations and inequalities.
        *   Example: Solving `2x + 3 = 7`.
    *   Polynomials: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and factoring.
        *   Factoring quadratic expressions: `x^2 + 5x + 6 = (x+2)(x+3)`.
    *   Systems of linear equations.
        *   Solving using substitution and elimination methods.

*   Set Theory
    *   Defining sets, subsets, and set operations (union, intersection, complement).
    *   Understanding Venn diagrams.
    *   Cartesian products of sets.

**II. Calculus**

*   Limits and Continuity
    *   Defining limits formally and informally.
    *   Calculating limits using algebraic techniques.
    *   Understanding continuity and its properties.
    *   Identifying types of discontinuities.

*   Differentiation
    *   Defining the derivative as a limit.
    *   Calculating derivatives using basic rules (power rule, product rule, quotient rule, chain rule).
        *   Example: Derivative of `x^3`: `3x^2`.
    *   Applications of derivatives: finding maxima, minima, and inflection points.
    *   Implicit differentiation.

*   Integration
    *   Defining the indefinite integral as an antiderivative.
    *   Calculating indefinite integrals using basic rules (power rule, substitution rule).
    *   Defining the definite integral as the area under a curve.
    *   The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
        *   Part 1: The derivative of the integral.
        *   Part 2: Evaluating definite integrals using antiderivatives.
    *   Applications of integration: finding areas, volumes, and average values.

**III. Linear Algebra**

*   Vectors and Matrices
    *   Defining vectors and matrices.
    *   Performing vector and matrix operations (addition, scalar multiplication, matrix multiplication).
    *   Understanding linear independence and span.

*   Systems of Linear Equations (revisited)
    *   Solving systems of linear equations using Gaussian elimination and matrix inversion.
    *   Understanding the concept of rank.

*   Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors
    *   Calculating eigenvalues and eigenvectors of a matrix.
    *   Understanding the importance of eigenvalues and eigenvectors in linear transformations.

*   Linear Transformations
    *   Representing linear transformations as matrices.
    *   Understanding the kernel and range of a linear transformation.

**IV. Probability and Statistics**

*   Basic Probability
    *   Defining probability, sample spaces, and events.
    *   Calculating probabilities using basic counting techniques (permutations and combinations).
    *   Conditional probability and Bayes' theorem.

*   Random Variables
    *   Defining discrete and continuous random variables.
    *   Probability distributions (binomial, Poisson, normal).
    *   Expected value and variance.

*   Statistical Inference
    *   Sampling distributions and the Central Limit Theorem.
    *   Confidence intervals.
    *   Hypothesis testing.

**V. Discrete Mathematics**

*   Logic and Proofs
    *   Propositional logic and predicate logic.
    *   Proof techniques: direct proof, proof by contradiction, proof by induction.

*   Combinatorics
    *   Counting principles: permutations, combinations, inclusion-exclusion principle.
    *   Generating functions.

*   Graph Theory
    *   Defining graphs and their properties (degree, connectivity, planarity).
    *   Graph traversal algorithms (depth-first search, breadth-first search).
    *   Spanning trees and shortest paths.

**VI. Advanced Topics (Examples)**

*   Real Analysis
    *   Rigorous treatment of limits, continuity, differentiation, and integration.
    *   Sequences and series.

*   Abstract Algebra
    *   Groups, rings, and fields.

*   Differential Equations
    *   Solving ordinary and partial differential equations.
