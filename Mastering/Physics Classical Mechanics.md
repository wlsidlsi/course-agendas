**I. Foundations of Classical Mechanics**

*   Understanding Basic Concepts
    *   Defining displacement, velocity, and acceleration.
    *   Understanding frames of reference and coordinate systems.
    *   Understanding units and dimensions; dimensional analysis.
*   Kinematics in One Dimension
    *   Analyzing motion with constant acceleration.
        *   Applying the equations of motion: `v = u + at`, `s = ut + (1/2)at^2`, `v^2 = u^2 + 2as`.
        *   Solving problems involving free fall.
    *   Analyzing motion with variable acceleration (introduction to calculus).
*   Vectors
    *   Understanding vector addition and subtraction (graphical and analytical methods).
    *   Understanding vector components and unit vectors (`i`, `j`, `k`).
    *   Performing scalar (dot) and vector (cross) products.

**II. Newtonian Mechanics**

*   Newton's Laws of Motion
    *   Understanding Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia).
    *   Applying Newton's Second Law (`F = ma`).
    *   Understanding Newton's Third Law (Action and Reaction).
*   Forces
    *   Identifying common forces: gravity, friction (static and kinetic), tension, normal force.
    *   Drawing free-body diagrams.
    *   Analyzing systems with multiple forces acting on them.
*   Work and Energy
    *   Defining work done by a constant force: `W = F * d * cos(theta)`.
    *   Understanding kinetic energy: `KE = (1/2)mv^2`.
    *   Understanding potential energy (gravitational and elastic).
    *   Applying the work-energy theorem.
*   Conservation of Energy
    *   Understanding conservative and non-conservative forces.
    *   Applying the principle of conservation of mechanical energy.
    *   Solving problems involving energy conservation with friction.
*   Momentum
    *   Defining linear momentum: `p = mv`.
    *   Understanding impulse and its relation to momentum change.
    *   Applying the principle of conservation of linear momentum.
    *   Analyzing collisions (elastic, inelastic, and perfectly inelastic).

**III. Rotational Motion**

*   Rotational Kinematics
    *   Defining angular displacement, angular velocity, and angular acceleration.
    *   Relating linear and angular quantities (`v = rω`, `a = rα`).
    *   Analyzing rotational motion with constant angular acceleration.
*   Torque and Rotational Inertia
    *   Defining torque: `τ = rFsin(θ)`.
    *   Calculating rotational inertia (moment of inertia) for simple objects.
        *   Example: `I = mr^2` for a point mass.
        *   Example: `I = (1/2)mr^2` for a solid cylinder.
    *   Applying Newton's Second Law for rotation: `τ = Iα`.
*   Rotational Kinetic Energy and Angular Momentum
    *   Understanding rotational kinetic energy: `KE = (1/2)Iω^2`.
    *   Defining angular momentum: `L = Iω`.
    *   Applying the principle of conservation of angular momentum.
*   Rolling Motion
    *   Analyzing rolling without slipping.
    *   Calculating the total kinetic energy of a rolling object.

**IV. Gravitation**

*   Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation
    *   Understanding the law: `F = G(m1m2)/r^2`.
    *   Calculating gravitational force between objects.
    *   Calculating the acceleration due to gravity.
*   Gravitational Potential Energy
    *   Understanding gravitational potential energy: `U = -G(m1m2)/r`.
    *   Calculating escape velocity.
*   Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion
    *   Understanding Kepler's three laws.
    *   Applying Kepler's laws to analyze planetary orbits.

**V. Oscillations and Waves**

*   Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)
    *   Understanding the conditions for SHM.
    *   Defining amplitude, frequency, period, and phase.
    *   Analyzing the motion of a mass-spring system.
    *   Analyzing the motion of a simple pendulum.
*   Damped Oscillations and Forced Oscillations
    *   Understanding the effects of damping on oscillations.
    *   Understanding resonance.
*   Wave Motion
    *   Understanding transverse and longitudinal waves.
    *   Defining wavelength, frequency, and wave speed.
        *   Relation: `v = fλ`.
    *   Understanding superposition and interference of waves.
*   Sound Waves
    *   Understanding the speed of sound.
    *   Understanding intensity and loudness.
    *   Understanding the Doppler effect.

**VI. Fluid Mechanics**

*   Fluid Statics
    *   Defining density and pressure.
    *   Understanding Pascal's principle.
    *   Understanding buoyancy and Archimedes' principle.
*   Fluid Dynamics
    *   Understanding viscosity.
    *   Understanding laminar and turbulent flow.
    *   Applying the equation of continuity: `A1v1 = A2v2`.
    *   Applying Bernoulli's equation.

**VII. Thermodynamics (Introduction)**

*   Temperature and Heat
    *   Understanding temperature scales (Celsius, Fahrenheit, Kelvin).
    *   Defining heat and specific heat capacity.
    *   Understanding heat transfer mechanisms (conduction, convection, radiation).
*   First Law of Thermodynamics
    *   Understanding internal energy.
    *   Applying the first law: `ΔU = Q - W`.
*   Ideal Gas Law
    *   Understanding the ideal gas law: `PV = nRT`.
    *   Calculating work done by an ideal gas.
*   Heat Engines and the Second Law of Thermodynamics
    *   Understanding heat engines and their efficiency.
    *   Understanding the second law of thermodynamics (entropy).
