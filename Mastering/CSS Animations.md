**III. Common Chart Types and Their Applications**

*   Bar charts and column charts.
    *   Comparing categorical data.
    *   Variations: grouped bar charts, stacked bar charts.
    *   Example: Visualizing sales by region using a bar chart.
*   Line charts.
    *   Showing trends over time.
    *   Using multiple lines to compare different datasets.
    *   Example: Tracking stock prices over a year using a line chart.
*   Pie charts and donut charts.
    *   Representing proportions of a whole.
    *   Limitations and when to avoid using them.
    *   Example: Showing market share distribution using a pie chart.
*   Scatter plots.
    *   Visualizing the relationship between two continuous variables.
    *   Identifying correlations and outliers.
    *   Example: Plotting advertising spend versus sales revenue using a scatter plot.
*   Histograms.
    *   Visualizing the distribution of a single continuous variable.
    *   Binning data and interpreting frequency distributions.
    *   Example: Showing the distribution of customer ages using a histogram.
*   Box plots (Box-and-Whisker plots).
    *   Summarizing the distribution of a dataset.
    *   Identifying quartiles, median, and outliers.
    *   Example: Comparing the distribution of test scores between two classes.
