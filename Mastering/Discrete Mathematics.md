**I. Foundations of Discrete Mathematics**

*   Understanding Sets and Set Operations
    *   Defining sets, subsets, power sets, and Cartesian products
    *   Performing set operations: union, intersection, complement, difference
    *   Examples: Representing data types as sets; set operations for data filtering.
*   Mastering Logic and Propositional Calculus
    *   Understanding propositions, logical connectives (AND, OR, NOT, IMPLIES, IFF)
    *   Constructing truth tables and evaluating logical expressions
    *   Equivalence, tautologies, and contradictions
    *   Applying logic to circuit design and program verification.
*   Exploring Predicate Logic
    *   Understanding predicates, quantifiers (universal and existential)
    *   Formulating and interpreting quantified statements
    *   Nested quantifiers and their order of evaluation
    *   Examples: Expressing mathematical statements and database queries in predicate logic.
*   Learning Proof Techniques
    *   Direct proof, proof by contraposition, proof by contradiction
    *   Proof by induction (base case, inductive hypothesis, inductive step)
    *   Examples: Proving properties of algorithms; proving mathematical identities.

**II. Combinatorics and Counting**

*   Understanding Basic Counting Principles
    *   The addition principle and the multiplication principle
    *   Applying these principles to solve simple counting problems
*   Mastering Permutations and Combinations
    *   Understanding permutations (ordered arrangements)
        *   Calculating `P(n, r)` = n! / (n-r)!
    *   Understanding combinations (unordered selections)
        *   Calculating `C(n, r)` = n! / (r! * (n-r)!)
    *   Examples: Counting passwords, selecting committees, arranging books on a shelf
*   Exploring Binomial Coefficients and Identities
    *   Understanding the binomial theorem: `(x + y)^n = Σ C(n, k) * x^(n-k) * y^k`
    *   Pascal's Identity: `C(n, k) = C(n-1, k-1) + C(n-1, k)`
    *   Applying binomial coefficients to probability and statistics
*   Solving Recurrence Relations
    *   Understanding linear homogeneous recurrence relations
    *   Finding characteristic roots and general solutions
    *   Examples: Fibonacci sequence, Tower of Hanoi

**III. Relations and Functions**

*   Understanding Relations and Their Properties
    *   Defining relations as sets of ordered pairs
    *   Properties of relations: reflexive, symmetric, transitive, antisymmetric
    *   Representing relations using matrices and directed graphs
*   Exploring Equivalence Relations and Partitions
    *   Defining equivalence relations and their properties
    *   Understanding equivalence classes and partitions
    *   Examples: Congruence modulo n; identifying equivalent software modules
*   Mastering Functions and Their Properties
    *   Defining functions, domain, codomain, and range
    *   Types of functions: one-to-one (injective), onto (surjective), bijective
    *   Composition of functions and inverse functions
*   Understanding Partial Orders and Lattices
    *   Defining partial orders and totally ordered sets
    *   Hasse diagrams for representing partial orders
    *   Lattices and their properties (join, meet)
    *   Examples: Subset relation; divisibility relation.

**IV. Graph Theory**

*   Understanding Basic Graph Concepts
    *   Defining graphs, vertices, and edges
    *   Types of graphs: directed, undirected, simple, multi-graphs
    *   Representing graphs: adjacency matrices, adjacency lists
*   Exploring Graph Connectivity
    *   Paths, cycles, and connected components
    *   Strongly connected and weakly connected components in directed graphs
    *   Depth-first search (DFS) and breadth-first search (BFS) algorithms for graph traversal
    *   Finding shortest paths using Dijkstra's algorithm and Bellman-Ford algorithm
*   Analyzing Trees and Spanning Trees
    *   Understanding trees and their properties
    *   Spanning trees and minimum spanning trees
    *   Algorithms for finding minimum spanning trees: Kruskal's algorithm, Prim's algorithm
*   Understanding Planar Graphs and Graph Coloring
    *   Planar graphs and Euler's formula: `v - e + f = 2`
    *   Graph coloring and chromatic number
    *   Applications: scheduling, resource allocation

**V. Abstract Algebra (Introduction)**

*   Understanding Algebraic Structures
    *   Defining groups, rings, and fields
    *   Binary operations and their properties (associativity, commutativity, identity, inverse)
*   Exploring Group Theory
    *   Subgroups, cyclic groups, and permutation groups
    *   Group homomorphisms and isomorphisms
*   Understanding Ring Theory
    *   Integral domains, fields, and polynomial rings
    *   Ring homomorphisms and ideals
*   Applying Algebraic Structures to Coding Theory
    *   Error-detecting and error-correcting codes
    *   Linear codes and cyclic codes
    *   Examples: Hamming codes, Reed-Solomon codes
