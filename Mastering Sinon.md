## Synonymy: A Graded Course Outline

**Week 1: Foundations**

*   Definition of Synonymy: Exploring exact, near, and partial synonyms.
*   Sources of Synonymy: Historical, regional, and stylistic variation.
*   Distinguishing Synonymy from Related Concepts: Polysemy, homonymy, hyponymy.

**Week 2: Levels of Synonymy**

*   Lexical Synonymy: Single words with similar meanings.
*   Phrasal Synonymy: Phrases or idioms conveying equivalent ideas.
*   Sentential Synonymy: Entire sentences expressing the same proposition.

**Week 3: Dimensions of Semantic Difference**

*   Denotation vs. Connotation: Core meaning vs. associated implications.
*   Register and Style: Formal vs. informal; literary vs. colloquial.
*   Emotional Tone: Positive, negative, or neutral connotations.

**Week 4: Context and Synonym Choice**

*   Collocation: Words that typically occur together; restricting synonym usage.
*   Register Maintenance: Matching the appropriate formality and style.
*   Audience Awareness: Choosing synonyms suitable for the intended reader/listener.

**Week 5: Practical Applications: Writing & Translation**

*   Avoiding Monotony: Strategic synonym use for stylistic variation.
*   Achieving Nuance: Selecting synonyms to convey precise shades of meaning.
*   Translation Challenges: Identifying and adapting synonyms across languages.

**Week 6: Advanced Topics**

*   Synonymy and Thesaurus Construction: Principles of organization and usage.
*   Synonymy in Computational Linguistics: Applications in NLP and information retrieval.
*   The Dynamic Nature of Synonymy: How meanings evolve and change over time.
