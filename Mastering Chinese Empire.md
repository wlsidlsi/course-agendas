# Chinese Empire: A Historical Progression

**Week 1: Geography & Pre-Imperial Foundations**

*   Geography of China: Impact on development & unity.
*   Early Dynasties: Xia, Shang, Zhou - Myth & reality.
*   The Mandate of Heaven & Dynastic Cycle.
*   Confucianism, Daoism, Legalism: Schools of thought.
*   The Warring States Period: Social & political upheaval.

**Week 2: The Qin Dynasty & Imperial Unification**

*   Rise of the Qin State: Military strength & Legalist policies.
*   Qin Shi Huangdi: Unification & centralization.
*   Legalism in practice: Standardizing weights, measures, script.
*   Construction projects: The Great Wall & Terracotta Army.
*   Fall of the Qin: Autocracy & rebellion.

**Week 3: The Han Dynasty: Consolidation & Expansion**

*   Rise of the Han: Liu Bang & the restoration of order.
*   Confucianism's Revival: Examination system & social order.
*   The Silk Road: Trade, cultural exchange, & expansion westward.
*   Technological advancements: Paper, iron casting, agriculture.
*   Social structure: Land ownership, peasantry, & merchants.

**Week 4: Division & Reunification: The Sui & Tang Dynasties**

*   The Three Kingdoms Period & the Northern & Southern Dynasties.
*   The Sui Dynasty: Reunification & ambitious projects.
*   The Grand Canal: Linking north and south.
*   The Tang Dynasty: Golden Age of Chinese civilization.
*   Buddhism's influence: Art, philosophy, and state patronage.

**Week 5: The Song Dynasty: Economic Revolution & Neo-Confucianism**

*   The Song Dynasty: Economic growth & urbanization.
*   Inventions & innovations: Printing, gunpowder, compass.
*   Neo-Confucianism: Ethical & metaphysical synthesis.
*   Foot binding: Social implications and gender roles.
*   External threats: Liao, Jin, and the eventual fall to the Mongols.

**Week 6: The Yuan Dynasty: Mongol Rule in China**

*   The Mongol Conquest: Genghis Khan & Kublai Khan.
*   The Yuan Dynasty: Foreign rule & administration.
*   Marco Polo & Western perspectives on China.
*   Religious tolerance & cultural exchange.
*   Rebellions & the fall of the Yuan.

**Week 7: The Ming Dynasty: Restoration & Exploration**

*   The Ming Dynasty: Overthrow of the Mongols & restoration of Chinese rule.
*   The Forbidden City: Symbol of imperial power.
*   Zheng He's Voyages: Maritime expeditions & trade.
*   Isolationism & the limits of Ming expansion.
*   The decline of the Ming: Internal corruption & external threats.

**Week 8: The Qing Dynasty: The Last Empire**

*   The Manchu Conquest: Rise of the Qing & expansion.
*   The Canton System: Trade restrictions & foreign relations.
*   Opium Wars: Western encroachment & unequal treaties.
*   Taiping Rebellion: Internal rebellion & social unrest.
*   Self-Strengthening Movement: Attempts at modernization.

**Week 9: Qing Dynasty Decline & Revolution**

*   Sino-Japanese War: Loss of territory & prestige.
*   Boxer Rebellion: Anti-foreign sentiment & imperial weakness.
*   The 1911 Revolution: Overthrow of the Qing Dynasty.
*   Sun Yat-sen & the establishment of the Republic of China.
*   Legacy of the Chinese Empire: Impact on modern China & the world.

**Week 10: Review and Conclusion**

*   Comprehensive review of key themes and periods.
*   Discussion of the enduring impact of the Chinese Empire.
*   Modern perspectives on Imperial Chinese history.
