# Chai 101: A Syllabus

**Week 1: Foundations**

*   Introduction: Chai - History, Culture, and Global Variations.
*   Basic Ingredients: Tea (types, sourcing), Milk (dairy/non-dairy), Sweetener (sugar, honey, etc.), Spices (cardamom, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, pepper).
*   Equipment: Kettle, saucepan, strainer, cups.
*   Basic Chai Recipe: Preparation and adjustments.

**Week 2: Spice Blending & Sourcing**

*   Spice Profiles: Understanding flavor characteristics of individual spices.
*   Spice Sourcing: Ethical considerations, quality indicators, and local options.
*   Spice Blending Techniques: Ratios, grinding, roasting (optional).
*   Creating Your Own Chai Masala: Experimentation and flavor balancing.

**Week 3: Tea Selection & Brewing Techniques**

*   Tea Varieties for Chai: Assam, Darjeeling, CTC (Crush, Tear, Curl).
*   Water Quality: Importance of pH and mineral content.
*   Brewing Methods: Simmering, steeping, decoction.
*   Tea-to-Water Ratio: Achieving desired strength and body.

**Week 4: Milk & Sweetener Dynamics**

*   Milk Types: Full fat, skim, almond, oat, soy.
*   Milk Steaming & Frothing: Techniques and considerations.
*   Sweetener Options: Sugar (white, brown, jaggery), honey, agave, maple syrup.
*   Sweetness Levels: Adjusting to personal preference and regional variations.

**Week 5: Regional Variations & Adaptations**

*   Indian Regional Styles: Cutting Chai (Mumbai), Kashmiri Kahwa, Masala Chai.
*   International Adaptations: Chai Lattes, Dirty Chai, Iced Chai.
*   Vegan Chai: Dairy-free alternatives and considerations.
*   Ayurvedic Principles in Chai: Balancing doshas with spices.

**Week 6: Advanced Techniques & Troubleshooting**

*   Chai Concentrate: Preparation and storage.
*   Perfecting the Pour: Achieving optimal texture and presentation.
*   Troubleshooting Common Issues: Bitterness, weak flavor, curdling.
*   Creating Signature Chai Blends: Developing unique recipes.

**Week 7: Chai & Food Pairing**

*   Flavor Profiles: Understanding complimentary and contrasting pairings.
*   Chai with Indian Sweets: Gulab Jamun, Jalebi, Barfi.
*   Chai with Baked Goods: Scones, biscuits, cakes.
*   Chai with Savory Foods: Paratha, samosas, pakoras.

**Week 8: The Art of Serving & Presentation**

*   Traditional Serving Styles: Kulhar cups, glass tumblers.
*   Modern Presentation: Latte art, garnishes, creative cups.
*   Chai Etiquette: Cultural considerations when serving and consuming.
*   Building a Chai Menu: Designing a selection for a café or home.
