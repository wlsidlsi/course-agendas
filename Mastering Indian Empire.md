## Indian Empire: Course Syllabus Outline

**Week 1: Introduction - The Pre-Empire Landscape**

*   Geographical Overview: Key Regions & Resources
*   Early Civilizations: Indus Valley, Vedic Period, Mauryan Empire (brief overview)
*   Regional Powers & Kingdoms: Pre-Mughal Context (e.g., Cholas, Delhi Sultanate)
*   Economic & Social Structures: Agriculture, Trade, Caste System (pre-Mughal)

**Week 2: The Mughal Empire - Foundation & Expansion**

*   Babur's Invasion & Establishment
*   Humayun's Struggles & Recovery
*   Akbar's Reign: Consolidation, Administration, Religious Policy (Din-i-Ilahi)
*   Jahangir & Shah Jahan: Patronage of Art & Architecture
*   Aurangzeb's Policies: Religious Conservatism & Expansion Challenges

**Week 3: Mughal Administration & Society**

*   Centralized Administration: Mansabdari System, Land Revenue System
*   Military Organization & Technology
*   Religious & Social Hierarchy: Role of Ulema, Sufis, Rajput Nobility
*   Economic Prosperity & Trade: Internal & External Networks
*   Art, Architecture & Culture: Mughal Contributions

**Week 4: Decline of the Mughal Empire**

*   Succession Wars & Weakening Central Authority
*   Religious & Regional Rebellions (e.g., Marathas, Sikhs)
*   Economic Stagnation & Fiscal Crisis
*   Invasions & Political Instability (e.g., Nadir Shah)
*   Rise of Regional Powers: Successor States

**Week 5: The Arrival of Europeans - Trade & Influence**

*   Early European Trading Companies: Portuguese, Dutch, English, French
*   Establishment of Trading Posts & Factories
*   Commercial Competition & Rivalries
*   Changing Economic Landscape: Impact on Indian Industries

**Week 6: The British East India Company - Rise to Power**

*   Formation & Early Activities of the British East India Company
*   Military Victories: Battle of Plassey, Battle of Buxar
*   Acquisition of Diwani Rights
*   Expansion of British Control: Annexation & Treaties

**Week 7: British Administration & Reforms**

*   Early Administrative Structures: Governor-Generals & Warren Hastings
*   Permanent Settlement & Land Revenue Policies
*   Judicial System & Legal Reforms
*   Military Reorganization & Recruitment
*   Social & Educational Reforms: Introduction of English Education

**Week 8: Indian Resistance to British Rule**

*   Early Resistance Movements: Peasant Uprisings & Tribal Rebellions
*   The Revolt of 1857: Causes, Course & Consequences
*   Rise of Nationalist Ideology & Organizations

**Week 9: The Rise of Indian Nationalism**

*   Early Nationalist Leaders & Thinkers
*   Formation of the Indian National Congress
*   Moderate & Extremist Factions within the Congress
*   Growth of Mass Movements: Swadeshi, Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience

**Week 10: Gandhi & The Indian Independence Movement**

*   Gandhi's Philosophy & Techniques of Satyagraha
*   Major Movements Led by Gandhi: Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience, Quit India
*   Role of Other Nationalist Leaders: Nehru, Patel, Bose
*   Political Negotiations & Constitutional Reforms

**Week 11: Partition & Independence**

*   The Demand for Pakistan & the Two-Nation Theory
*   Negotiations for Independence & the Partition Plan
*   The Partition of India: Violence, Displacement & Refugee Crisis
*   India's Independence & the Legacy of Partition

**Week 12: Post-Independence India - Key Challenges & Developments**

*   Integration of Princely States
*   Framing of the Indian Constitution
*   Economic Development & Planning
*   Social & Political Issues
*   India's Role in International Affairs (brief overview)
